A Few Things I Want To Share....

Fall is quickly upon us, and I cannot believe how quickly this year has gone!  With so many folks having record setting months for their businesses throughout this year, I look forward to the momentum we can generate through the fall and into the new year.
 
Now is the time to develop a solid marketing plan to ensure you stay in front of your real estate agents through becoming a strategic partner of theirs by supporting their business goals.  A wise person once said, "If you help enough people reach their goals, you will exceed your own."  If you are working with one agent, focus on getting to know their co-workers and fellow agents.  Educate and support their business goals while touching more agents through lunch and learn sessions, and ask for an opportunity to showcase the Pillar To Post way through a referral.  Now is the time to plant the seeds for a blossoming spring!
 
As we get into the fourth quarter of 2019, I respectfully suggest you look at a few things:
  1. Since it is typically the slowest quarter of the year, drive strong relationship-based marketing. It will help push for a strong finish for the year and lay tremendous groundwork to have a strong start to 2020.
     
  2. Work on your plan for 2020. By now you can relatively predict where you will finish for 2019. Use those numbers to help you decide where you want to be at the end of 2020. Set personal targets for yourself on what you want to drive towards - sales, inspections, upgrade rate, additional services or Listen 360 scores. Make sure you also set a plan for your marketing activity. Know what you need to do each month or week so that you hit your goals!

  3. Try at least one new marketing initiative you haven't tried before. Commit some effort to it. Think beyond what you typically do. What can you do today that you haven't tried, that your customers and agents would love and truly find valuable?
A number of you have reached out to work with me one-on-one in looking at your business plans for 2020.  I'm happy to have the opportunity to work with each of you one on one and help you create a plan to achieve your goals.  Please feel free to reach out and let me know if you would like to set up a time to discuss and review your plans for 2020.

More Value...For THEM!  A New Way To Think About The Way You Present
 
When it comes to marketing a service it can at times be more challenging than marketing a product.  You are not selling something that is tangible; you are in  fact selling the invisible.

- Where is "value" in your sales equation?
- Where is "value" in your sales presentation?
- How does your prospect PERCEIVE what you're saying and offering is actually valuable to THEM?
 
ANSWER :  Your value proposition needs to define value
 in terms of the customer (in other words - CUSTOMER CENTRIC).
 
Giving value and adding value are words that many of us have a diffic ult time understanding, let alone providing.   Most people think that value is all about something the company adds.  Some small additional service, something tacked onto the product, a slight reduction in price, even something "free."  Wrong.
V alue is something done for the customer, i n favor of th e  cu st o m e r.    T h e  r ight value proposition will: engage, gain intere st,  el iminat e   pric e as    an o bjection, e lim inate bidding, eliminate competition, and do uble your sales.  

And it can ONLY be accomplished by PROVING value.

There's an old clichÃ© in sales that goes, "They don't care how much you know until they know how much you care."  That statement is wrong. It should read, "They don't care how much you know unless they know where the productivity is, where the benefit is, where the risk reduction is, and where the profit is. In short, they want to know what's in it for them and where the value is.  They have to PERCEIVE value for them.  And, in short, if you don't spell that value out, I guarantee that you will fight a price war.  And even if you win the war, you will lose your profit.

-Jeffrey Gitomer

3 Ways To Dramatically Improve Referral Marketing
 
It's a fact:  People would rather do business with people they know--or know of--than with strangers.  When you're introduced to a prospect through a personal recommendation, that prospect has a vastly higher comfort level than, say, a buyer you find through cold calling/emailing.  After all, few things are more reassuring than a positive endorsement from someone you know and trust.
So why is it that, while we all covet referrals, we don't pursue them as much as we could and should?  I think it's largely a matter of developing good habits.

1.  Change Your Thinking

Imagine your business as an infinite web of relationships.  Every one of your business contacts has the potential to connect you to dozens of other contacts.  The relationships are out there, but they'll likely remain out of reach unless you actively pursue them.  It may never occur to your current contacts to broker an introduction.  It's up to you to put the idea in their heads.

Don't feel sheepish about asking for referrals; there's nothing pushy or shady about it.  People won't give you referrals unless you deserve them, and even then, often not until you ask for them.  In fact, getting a referral is the highest compliment you can receive.  Let your customers and agents know you prize referrals, which you'll earn by providing excellent quality inspections and additional services.  If anyone in any industry understands the importance of referrals, its real estate agents - it is the premise of their business.  So be proud of the fantastic service that you provide and be sure to ask your happy agents to introduce you to one of their peers!

2.  Make It a Habit

I know one entrepreneur who built a successful business almost solely on referrals.  How'd he get so good at it?  When he was an eager young sales apprentice, his manager trained him well.  Every time he glanced at his watch, which he did often in his zeal to stay on schedule, it meant it was time to ask for a referral.  Eventually, it became second nature.

Here are more easy ways to start developing good referral-building habits:
  • When you begin working with a new agent, make referrals part of your initial agreement.  "If I do a great job for you - and I will - I would sincerely appreciate you giving me a few referrals to other busy, professional agents like yourself."  Chances are your agent will be impressed by your dedication and drive. 
  • Whenever a customer compliments you, respond with a thank you, quickly followed by a referral request.  For example, "I'm so pleased you're happy with my work.  Do you know any other agents who can benefit from my services?
  • Use every client meeting as an opportunity to collect referrals.  To keep yourself on track, jot a reminder down in your schedule or inspection preparation notes.  Make it one of your standard talking points.
  • Set a weekly goal for yourself.  Keep track of the number of referrals you ask for each day.  You don't need to limit your requests to agents; you can also ask customers, business associates, acquaintances and prospects.
  • Make the most of every networking opportunity.  Step out of your comfort zone at networking events and set a goal to talk to at least three new people.  Plan in advance what you might say.  We're all drawn to interesting, enthusiastic people.
  • Always be specific when asking for a referral.  Remember, you are targeting busy professional realtors - make sure you let them know that.  If you don't tell your contacts who your target customer is, you'll waste time pursuing leads you can't use or don't want.
3.  Give and You Shall Receive
One of the most powerful ways to elicit referrals is to give them generously yourself.  Whenever you have the opportunity to refer an associate, buyer or to bring two contacts together, do so.  And when you're attending the aforementioned networking event, make a point of introducing people to one another.  Most people will appreciate the referral, and it will inspire them to respond in kind.
One last thought:  Always thank someone who has given you a referral.  Send them a note, keep them informed of your progress and maybe even treat them to lunch.
What's the close ratio on referral business, compared to other prospecting methods?  I don't have a definitive answer for you, but I've seen estimates that range from 50 to 500 percent more.  Those are big numbers.  Whatever that number is for you, you can bet it's a whole lot higher than cold calling, advertising, web marketing or virtually any other sales technique you might employ.
The referral is the number-one tool in your tool kit.  Get in the habit of reaching for it often-you will reap the rewards.

Brand Experience: Marketing Yourself at Open Houses with the Right Survival Kit

I am often asked, "What are some things other franchisees are having success with in Marketing?"  I can share that one thing I am hearing a lot of success stories around lately are attending open houses.  Below are a couple of samples of "Open House Survival Kits."



A majority of open houses occur over the weekend.  However, if you check Zillow or Redfin, you'll find several on weekdays.  Take a moment to see where they are and if you can stop by around your inspection schedule during the week too.  Weekday open houses are often poorly attended by buyers, which can give you more opportunities to speak with agents.  If the open house has the assistant to "the big agent" there, don't fret, that's often the person that books the inspections.

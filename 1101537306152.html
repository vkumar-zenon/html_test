United Way of Central Georgia makes NATIONAL headlines with hometown Santa hero
After years of mistaken phone calls, Macon native, Scott Chalkley, used his collection of voicemails left for Santa to raise money for The Merrie Christmas Project. It all started about eight years ago when Google listed a number sharing 7 digits in common with Chalkley as the number to reach Santa. Over the years, the calls started coming in from kids in Central Georgia leaving their wish lists on Chalkley's voicemail. Through the years, Chalkley started to embrace the role, eventually changing his voicemail to the Santa hotline voicemail.

This year, he decided to launch a website to put the voicemail collection on display and to find a unique way to raise money for United Way of Central Georgia's newest initiative, The Merrie Christmas Project.

We shared the story of Chalkey with media near and far and the story got picked up by the CBS Evening News as the main feature story (kicker) of the newscast . We've since heard the story has aired on more TV stations across the country... all featuring the Santa from Macon and the United Way of Central Georgia.

Closing the gap of food insecurity in Central Georgia
Food insecurity is a problem all communities face. Many people, including children, worry about where they will find their next meal. Here at United Way of Central Georgia, we are working to help bridge this gap. 14.1% of people in Georgia are food insecure, but 17.7% of Central Georgians are food insecure. Some of the most startling statistics are in Hancock County where nearly 1 in 4 people have trouble accessing food.

UWCG has recently collaborated with One World Link and AmeriCorps to bring in service member, Will Finkelstein, to help identify the food insecurity problem in Central Georgia and work towards a solution. Finkelstein has been meeting with area churches and organizations particularly in the Pleasant Hill community to help establish relationships and build a plan to get a more accessible route to nutritious food for our food desert communities.

Watch this video to see some of the hands at work to provide food to those in our community that need it.
UWCG assumes operational responsibility of Family Counseling Center
United Way of Central Georgia will assume management and operational responsibility for Family Counseling Center of Central Georgia. The relationship dates to 1957 when United Way of Central Georgia and the Junior League started FCC. Over the years, Family Counseling Center of Central Georgia grew as its own 501c3 offering professional counseling services in Central Georgia including crime victim counseling, individual, marital, and family counseling, adoption and custody evaluations, family in transition services, and more. As demand for services continued to grow, like most nonprofits, the funding did not grow along with the need and growth. At the announcing of Frank Macks (current FCC Executive Director) retirement, FCCs Board of Trustees engaged an outside consultant, and their recommendation as to the best course of action was to merge with United Way.

Mercer women's basketball hosts "Teddy bear toss" to benefit UWCG
Over 100 stuffed toys were collected at the Mercer women's basketball game on December 15th and will be donated to children in Central Georgia through United Way of Central Georgia initiative, The Merrie Christmas Project. United Way of Central Georgia, alongside Mike and Nancy Kaplan, parents of the late Merrie Kaplan, unveiled The MERRIE Christmas Project in honor of Merries love for Christmas a few months ago. The 45-year old Macon woman, Merrie Kaplan, was born with a rare genetic condition, Williams syndrome. Fitting to her name, she was known throughout central Georgia as a friendly, outgoing woman, who brought joy wherever she went. Merrie passed away this past August, but her memory will live on and bring joy across United Way of Central Georgias 14-county service area through The MERRIE Christmas Project, a fundraising effort aimed at providing gifts, holiday dcor, and food to families in need during the Christmas season.

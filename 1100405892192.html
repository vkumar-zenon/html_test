A Note from Catherine
2019 was an amazing year at Big Sky Ranch/CATNIP Foundation.
As I reflect on 2019, I am grateful for all the happy endings, forever homes, and lives saved. And yet, over the last 7 years we have only begun to scratch the surface of meeting the needs of the animals in our community. Our experience shows that access to low-cost veterinary care is key to turning the tide of pet overpopulation, and the rural communities we serve are desperate for it. In between adoptions, transports, food distribution and high volume spay/neuter efforts, we have been working hard to secure the resources to expand our lifesaving clinic. In 2020, our focus will be completing the build-out of our new space at 82060 Highway 25 in Folsom.
When it opens, this new facility will allow us to multiply our available spay/neuter services, operate a cat adoption cafe, and generate new revenue streams with space for retail and boarding. Beyond expanding available services, the large parking lot and green space on the property make it a perfect spot for engaging the community through festive outreach and enlightening education events. Please donate or sign up to volunteer for remodeling work, painting, or cleaning. We won't be able to finish this project and open our new facility without your help!
As we turn our focus to expanding CATNIP's capacity to keep the most vulnerable animals out of shelters, a recent rescue trip is weighing heavily on my mind. I was invited by one of our out-of-state partner rescue organizations to assist with recovering 58 neglected dogs from a single site in desolate San Angelo, Texas. It was the site of a beautiful, welcoming, highly functioning animal shelter , but things had taken a turn. A culture indifferent to the disposability of animals turned this place turned into a dungeon for those it was designed and intended to help. More and more animals were dumped at the site until the director was completely overwhelmed with the demands for care and unable to dedicate time to generating resources. To make matters worse, the director developed health problems of his own. His focus was always the animals, and eventually they all suffered drastically without the resources required to keep them all healthy. You can learn more about the Rural Texas 58 here .
My experience in Texas affected me deeply. I returned from Texas with 7 dogs who were some of the oldest and most challenging cases of unintentional neglect to come out of this terrible situation. They are now CATNIP dogs, and it is our mission to see to it that they never suffer again. I put some of my thoughts into a Facebook post you might have seen. As we move into 2020, my resolve is even stronger to build a community where resources are available to keep animals in homes and out of shelters. I can't do that without your continued support. If you can help our clinic expansion efforts with in-kind donations of building materials, commercial washer/dryers, cabinets, countertops, glass, or labor, please contact me at cwilbert@bigskyranch.org .

With the Texas visit always in my thoughts, I will be embarking on a major fundraising campaign to put our clinic expansion over the finish line in 2020. I am counting on you to join with me in building a community where the value of every animal is affirmed. We can do this if we do it together. There are so many ways you can help! Below are just a few. I look forward to working with you in 2020 to keep changing the ways we think and feel about living with and caring for animals.

With gratitude,
Catherine

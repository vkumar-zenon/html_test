Happy New Year!
        Happy New Year!  We turn the page on Wednesday. This year saw a severe lack of inventory and which resulted in higher home prices. The cards were stacked against the first time homebuyer. Fortunately  first timers had an ace in the hole. Mortgage interest rates remained low all year.
       Maybe 2020 will be the year of new residential construction. We need the inventory. Without a substantial increase of inventory we will not be able to sustain the higher home prices and first timers will be priced out of the market.
      For over ten years now I have used this space to tell you that this could be the year that we finally see mortgage interest rates at 5% or higher. I have been wrong and now we are entering an election year. I expect mortgage interest rates to stay relatively low.
      Happy New Year! I hope 2020 turn out just the way you want it to.           
10 years ago............
         Here we are at the end of 2019. We are also at the end of a decade. A lot has changed. Facebook had eclipsed Myspace by 2010 but Myspace was still a bigger presence than Twitter. Snapchat, Instagram, and Pinterest hadn't even been launched. 
       You probably still had a home phone. Uber was around but you probably had yet to try the service. Lyft wasn't even started until 2012. Most offices still had fax machines. If you wanted to know what the weather was going to be like you had to turn on the television instead of asking Alexa or Siri. All new cars had CD players in them..........and most still had cassette decks.
        Zillow was founded in 2006 but it was just a website for the general public to get inaccurate information about their home's value. You could "Ring" a doorbell but you probably couldn't see a real time video of who was outside your front door.
       Amazon was around but no one had ever seen an Amazon delivery van because Amazon was still shipping through FedEx and UPS. Black Friday happened on Friday and most of your Christmas shopping took place at the mall. On your way home from a hard day of shopping you could stop at Blockbuster Video and pick out a movie to watch. 
        I miss Blockbuster Video. Blockbuster was in a perfect position to become Netflix. Blockbuster had the market cornered. Literally. They had the names and phone numbers of millions of people that were willing to pay for entertainment in their homes. The transition would have been gradual and smooth. 
        Sears could have been Amazon! Sears already had a catalog that could have been transitioned to a website. Can you imagine the bankroll that Jeff Bezos would have needed to compete? Sears could have shipped ordered merchandise to their retail stores and dispatched home deliveries from there. Sears already had the infrastructure!
       What will 2030 look like for the real estate business? Opendoor has a vision. Zillow is transforming. You and I may have to adjust our business models. We may have to change the way we serve our clients. We still have a place in the real estate market because some things will not change. Home buyers will still want to buy and homeowners will still need to sell and I believe they are willing to pay for professional service. You and I are still better at selling homes and making loans than the web-based competition.
       We may have to change in the 2020's. We can and we will.             
 
Interest Rate Update
 
       As expected the market was quiet last week. Mortgage interest rates were almost unchanged from the previous week. I don't expect major movement this week either. It's next week that could bring changes. We will be out of the holiday season and the people that make decisions that affect the market will be back at work doing market changing things.
      I think mortgage interest rates will change next week but no one knows which direction they will move or how much they will change. My best guess is that interest rates fall slightly.             
       Here are the latest results from  Freddie's mortgage interest rate survey:

-30 year fixed rate 3.74% with 0.7% discount points
-15 year fixed rate 3.19% with 0.7% discount points
-5 year adjustable rate 3.45% with 0.3% discount points    
                                                      
Did you know?
         Do you know what the biggest obstacle is for first time home buyers? It isn't credit. Americans are boosting their credit scores, which bodes well for applicants seeking a mortgage to snag the lowest rates. The average credit score hit its highest level since 2011, reaching 682--two points higher than a year ago, Experian reports in its State of Credit report. Consumers also are taking on more mortgage debt than a year ago, and delinquency rates are decreasing too.  
       Comparing borrowing behaviors between genders, women's average credit scores are four points higher than men's (686 vs. 682, respectively). By generation, Gen Xers, millennials, and Gen Zers tend to carry more debt than older generations.
        For the record, the biggest obstacle facing first time home buyers
these days is the down payment. We can solve that problem!

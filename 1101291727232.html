Tonight! New Year's Eve Comedy at CinemaSalem   7:30 & 9:30
Cinema Salem hosts New Year's Eve Comedy with double headliner bill.

CinemaSalem will host its first-ever New Year's Eve Comedy Night on Tuesday, December 31 with a double-headliner bill featuring Boston comedians Mark Scalia and Dave Rattigan, along with host Heather Peabody.

"We've wanted to do New Year's Eve Comedy for quite a while," CinemaSalem owner Paul Van Ness said. "We think it's a great entertainment option for our audience. It will also be convenient for people who want something to do after dinner, or before dinner or drinks in downtown Salem."

Presented by Scamps Comedy Productions and CinemaSalem, 1 East India Square in Salem, the shows will start at 7:30 and 9:30 p.m. Tickets are $25, available at the cinema, or online at cinemasalem.com or scampscomedy.com. There will be a cash bar.

"New Year's Eve is a great night for comedy," said Rattigan, comedian and Scamps Comedy owner. "Coming together to share a laugh is a great way to symbolically finish the year and hit your emotional reset button for 2020. Salem is a great city for entertainment, so it's going to be a ball."

Scalia has headlined across the United States and Canada at major comedy clubs, numerous colleges and universities, along with corporate, private, and religious venues, but what many will know him best for locally is his acting role as Scrooge on the Salem Trolley during the holiday season. As an actor, he works stages, television, in films, and as a corporate improv specialist. He has also performed at The Boston International Comedy Festival, Seattle International Comedy Festival and the New Orleans Comedy Festival. He's also performed standup with the show How Men Think (Or Do They?).  

Rattigan is a Boston comedian whose CD has been played on Sirius Satellite Radio and various radio stations and podcasts. In addition to top clubs and theaters, he has appeared on commercials and on the syndicated Steve Katsos Show, and has written humorous pieces for publications including The Boston Globe, Northshore magazine, and The Christian Science Monitor. Formerly an award-winning journalist, he's also appeared in the independent 2011 film Heavy Times, lent his voice to an animated feature expected to be released in 2020, and written a short film that will make the rounds of the festival circuit in 2020. He's performed with such diverse national acts as Jeff Dunham, Steven Wright, Nick DiPaolo and The Beach Boys.

Peabody is the tallest female comedian on the New England comedy circuit, and a rising star. She's worked with headliners Juston McKinney, Tony V., Jimmy Dunn, Patty Ross, Frank Santorelli, Mike McDonald and Paul Nardizzi, all of whom she's taller than. In her "other" professional life, she's a mental health expert specializing in dementia, which she has prepared her well for dealing with comedians.

For more information, go to CinemaSalem.com or ScampsComedy.com.

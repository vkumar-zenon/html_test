Meet, network and connect with mentors and colleagues!

TeamMates is a networking initiative of the American Academy of Sports Physical Therapy of the APTA. The purpose is to help new and experienced members of the AASPT connect for discussions, introductions, mentoring, and networking opportunities at conferences.
 
When you pre-register, you may participate as a "rookie" or a "captain" in a TeamMate group.
 
Rookies:
  • New sports PTs and PTAs in the first 5 years of practice
  • Current students
  • Professionals with a recent career change to sports physical therapy in past 2 - 3 years
  • Rookies may be new AASPT members or those interested in learning more about the Academy.
Captains:
  • AASPT members with 5+ years of experience in sports physical therapy
  • AASPTmembers who have completed a sports residency or fellowship or currently hold their SCS
  • Participants should be willing to meet new or first time members and foster introductions between other AASPT members.
Pre-registered rookies and captains receive contact information via email prior to attending TCC or CSM to "meet" each other virtually in advance of the meeting.

At the meeting, Rookies are seated with their Captains for face to face connection and discussion in a relaxed setting. You'll also have the opportunity to meet others in your peer group and in the Captain peer group, expanding your sports physical therapy network.

These events are of limited size and always fill up completely, so register early!

AASPT ANNOUNCES PARTNERED SYMPOSIA TO ENHANCE MEMBER EDUCATION OPPORTUNITIES   
 
 
THE SCIENCE OF RUNNING MEDICINE
 
April 25-26, 2020  |  Mayo Clinic, Rochester, MN

$550 per person

$475 for American Academy of Sports Physical Therapy Members

$400 for students

$340 for American Academy of Sports Physical Therapy Member Students
This evidence-based symposium will bring together top clinical researchers in the field of biomechanics to discuss and debate contemporary topics related to running biomechanics, running-related injuries, and the rehabilitation of the injured runner. The use of video analysis as part of the running gait examination will be emphasized, and novel strategies to modify running mechanics will be presented as part of a comprehensive approach to treatment. Multiple case studies will be integrated to illustrate concepts and promote idea application. Participants will be provided with practical information to "take back" to the clinic for immediate use in managing the injured runner.

Examination & Treatment of Running Injuries 
This advanced 2-day course will provide a thorough review of the most recent scientific findings related to the examination, diagnosis and treatment of running injuries. Discussion will focus on the current literature pertaining to running mechanics and injury, and the clinical decision making needed to achieve superior outcomes. The use of video analysis as part of the running gait examination will be emphasized, and novel strategies to modify running mechanics will be presented as part of a comprehensive approach to treatment. The overall objective is to provide each participant with an understanding of the current knowledge pertaining to the management of running-related injuries, with techniques that can be immediately applied to clinical practice.

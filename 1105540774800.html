At PECO, we prepare for potential outages caused by storms long before inclement weather hits. However, severe storms and other unpredictable events have impacted our region, causing damage to electrical equipment and disrupting the flow of power to homes and businesses. When power goes out, our restoration process begins. PECO is committed to restoring service as quickly as possible, while maintaining the safety of our customers and employees as the top priority.
When an outage is widespread, restoring power to all affected customers at the same time may not be possible, so our crews follow an established protocol to help ensure public safety while returning customers to service. PECOâs sequence for restoring power is as follows:
 
1) Affected substations  

2) Facilities critical to public health and safety â including hospitals, police and fire stations, water treatment plants and communications systems.

3) Equipment that will return service to the largest number of customers in the least amount of time, including major distribution and overhead lines, high-density housing developments and large neighborhoods. 

4) Smaller neighborhoods, transformers, and individual homes or businesses

Encourage Customers to
Apply for Available Energy Assistance Programs 
PECO reminds customers, who are struggling to pay their bills, to take advantage of LIHEAP, the Low-Income Home Energy Assistance Program. LIHEAP provides cash grants, up to $1,000 per customer, through a direct payment to authorized vendors, like PECO. Customers do not have to be behind on their bills in order to receive the cash grant.

LIHEAP also has money allocated for emergencies, including payments of up to $600, to reinstate service that has been shut off due to non-payment and for repairing leaking pipes or broken furnaces. The grants are based on household size, income, the type of fuel used, and county of residence.

Customers can call 1-800-34 HELP-4 or visit www.peco.com/liheap , for questions or help with filling out the application. For more information on the LIHEAP program, please visit, www.peco.com/liheap .
 
PECO manages the largest and most comprehensive low-income assistance program portfolio in Pennsylvania. In addition to LIHEAP, PECO has many other programs to help customers manage their energy costs and save money. Other assistance programs include:

Customer Assistance Program (CAP): This program provides a credit based on household income and energy use.

Matching Energy Assistance Fund (MEAF): A hardship program funded by voluntary contributions of customers and matched dollar for dollar by PECO for up to $1,000 in total assistance for those who qualify.

Low Income Usage Reduction Program (LIURP): A program that provides year-round conservation and weatherization assistance for qualified households.

Be Aware of Scams During
the Holiday Season
With the arrival of the holiday season, PECO is reminding customers to always be on alert for potential payment scams targeting utility customers. Scams occur throughout the year, but the company has seen an increase in scam reports during the holiday season. 

RED FLAGS FOR SCAM ACTIVITY
  • The scammer often becomes aggressive and tells a customer his or her account is past due and service will be shut off if a large payment isnât made â usually within less than an hour.
  • The scammer instructs the customer to purchase a prepaid debit or credit card â widely available at most retail stores â then call him or her back to supposedly make a payment.
  • The scammer asks the customer for the prepaid cardâs receipt number and PIN number, which grants instant access to the funds loaded to the card.
 
HOW TO PROTECT YOURSELF
  • Utility representatives will never ask or require a customer with a past due balance to purchase a prepaid debit card to avoid disconnection.
  • Customers can make payments online, by phone, automatic bank withdraw, mail or in person.
  • Customers with a past due balance will receive multiple shut off notifications â never a single notification one hour before disconnection.
 
Any PECO customer who believes he or she has been a target of a scam is urged to contact their local police and call PECO immediately at 1-800-494-4000

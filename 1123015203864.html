The Year In Review:
Our Advocacy efforts this year began with a letter to the Ontario Minister of Health to bring attention to the gaps in neurosurgical care. In addition, meetings took place with medical advisers every other month to discuss strategy and to facilitate important connections with international specialists in this field of medicine. As an overlap in advocacy and annual conferences, we participated as a community sponsor providing in-kind support, in the planning and organization of the CSF Flow at Niagara Falls Conference with the Column of Hope. The conference was held in June where the outcomes of brokering key introductions came to life with over 95 specialist neurosurgeons in attendance from five continents. During our own conference in November, we heard from Dr. Hance Clarke who stated in his presentation that discussions are taking place to address the neurosurgical level of care. All this to say, we know the gaps still exist broadly across Canada, let alone Ontario.

Our November conference was quite literally the best one we have had since our first event held in 2013. Existing and new volunteers Susan Edwards and Randy Klein who are members of the conference planning committee, made tremendous impact in creative and meaningful ways. i.e.: creating a Vendor and Exhibitor fee structure program to help us expand the kinds of resources we can provide to people that attend. This exciting detail also helped us to cover the growing costs of running the conference and protect and build upon the Garden Party Conference Designated Fund. We worked with our healthcare professional advisers to set the educational program with tremendous support from Christina Pridmore this year. We also worked with representatives at SickKids, where this year they provided the venue at the Peter Gilgan Centre for Research and Learning, charging us only their costs for manpower & catering. New in 2019, was adding the capability of offering web-based attendance, which we hope to be able to offer in the coming years. Beyond attendees from Ontario, healthcare professionals and patients attended from Quebec, Alberta. Please read our full conference report on our website at www.theilcfoundation.org/annual-conference/

Our peer support network continues to grow organically online as opposed to hosting in-person meetings. There are new volunteers that are working to organize small group "Meet-ups" in communities where they live, with the goal to organically grow over time. To grow this area of support in many communities cross-provincially, we would need a broader volunteer base. When this happens, the goal would be to secure volunteers that would be interested in expanding the program to include ILC Care Packages, with the intention of delivering the packages/items through local hospitals or directly to people that reach out to us for support (posting packages to them).

A great deal of work went in to completely overhauling our website this year with tremendous organizational support from Mary Steckner and Bev Stofko, our webmaster. Resources have been reorganized to clearly reflect what we do, why we do what we do and who we support. Feedback from multiple stakeholders has been excellent. There has been an increase of healthcare professionals, patients, caregivers alike, that have called to ask specific questions about resources listed. Compliments on ease of use, packed with information they have not been able to find elsewhere, especially for those interested in resources from a Canadian charity. A major area of need is to have the website translated in to French. The population in Quebec certainly experience the same level of marginalized or no care, but added to this is the language barrier. My hope is to identify a funder in Quebec to fund this priority initiative.

Again, thank you for all that you have done and are doing to help make a difference for the population we serve.

Wishing each of you the very best of the Holiday Season and health, happiness and good fortune in the New Year!

Sandy Smeenk
Executive Director

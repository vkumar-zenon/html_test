2019 may be ending but Harlem Needle Arts is just getting started as we share our most recent public art installation We the People | Disrupting Silence at Col. Charles Young Triangle Park on 153rd Street and Adam Clayton Powell Jr. Blvd.

The installation features knit design and crochet work by Nacinimod Deodee and Sounds of Reflection by DJ LotusMoon.

The exhibit pays tribute to the ingenuity, creativity and sacrifices of Africans of the Diaspora, who suffered the atrocity of enslavement, marginalization and disenfranchisement. Through this body of work we honor our past, present and the plans for the future.

The work allows space for reflections, honesty and visioning for our future.

I do hope you will make time to visit the park with your family and friends, be in the natural landscape of our Harlem park and listen and view We the People | Disrupting Silence.

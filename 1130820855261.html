Homeless Motions
In early December I introduced two motions to provide additional shelter for those experiencing homelessness in DTLAâs Skid Row. The first instructs the City Council to approve funding to ensure the construction of a bridge housing facility at 310 North Main Street in Downtown Los Angeles.
 
A second motion introduced requests an additional $14 Million in funding that will be used to fund additional bridge housing facilities. Skid Row is the epicenter of the housing crisis in the City of Los Angeles with approximately 3,000 people sleeping on the streets every night.
 
The two motions were introduced on the same day another motion I had introduced was approved by the City Council to provide $6 Million for Skid Row from the State of California Homeless Housing, Assistance, and Prevention Program (HHAP).
 
In February 2019, the Los Angeles City Council approved an emergency expenditure of $20 million dollars in Homeless Emergency Assistance Program (HEAP) from the State of California. Due to the urgent need, all of these funds have been expended. These expenditures focused on temporary crisis and bridge housing, storage, and hygiene programs. The funding greatly increased these services in a triage response that focused on addressing the most urgent issues immediately while working toward the long term goal of permanent housing for all.
 
Through a partnership with community stakeholders and city departments, my team has been successful in providing an emergency response for Skid Row. The additional funding will be used to provide needed bridge housing as the next step in the triage response that will provide beds, shelter and access to social services with the goal of moving the individuals toward long term housing.
City Takes Key Step Toward Youth Development Department
Young people are one of the greatest assets in the City of Los Angeles. With investment in programs and training, youth in under-served areas are better prepared for higher education and the workforce. The City of Los Angeles does not currently have a centralized approach to youth development and the motion I co-authored with Councilmember Monica Rodriguez is the first step in rectifying that situation. It will guarantee a more cohesive and coordinated approach.

The motion instructs the City Administrative Officer and the Chief Legislative Analyst to create an Executive Task Force on Youth Development. The Task Force will assemble a report outlining specific steps and detailed funding requirements necessary to implement a Youth Development Strategy.

Giving young people the possibility to better themselves will be life changing. It is my hope that it will allow them to fulfill dreams they may have thought were otherwise unattainable.

The composition of the Task Force will include city officials, representatives from youth-service organizations working in high-need communities, and young people willing to advocate for the concerns of high-need youth.

NELA Homeless Connect Day
On December 5th Team Huizar sponsored a Homeless Connect Day in Eagle Rock. I want to thank Exodus Recovery for the partnership and we look forward to providing more Connect Days throughout Council District 14 in 2020.

Homeless Connect Days are a one-stop shop connecting people experiencing homelessness with a number of organizations, many of whom are on the front lines of our fight against homelessness. These events give people access to housing, mental health and hygiene services, employment programs and other service providers. Service providers connect attendees with housing resources for families, veterans, single adults and transitional age youth.

There were also providers that focus on mental health, substance issues, health care, domestic violence and offer free legal help. Individuals were able to enroll in programs including Medical, Cal Fresh, Social Security and Metro Discount programs as well as receiving free haircuts, lunch, ID vouchers, health screenings, vaccines and more.

Funding for 710 Improvements
In December, I introduced a motion to finalize the funding for Phase 2 of the 710 multi-modal mobility improvements project that the Metro Board approved this fall. This project will bring $100 million worth of much needed improvements to our communities of El Sereno and Northeast Los Angeles. Such projects will help reduce traffic congestion and increase safety along our most congested and major corridors.

The funding will allow for projects that have gone through the visioning process and community outreach to move forward. My office is committed to moving the funding aspect forward quickly so that the implementation of these projects can begin and the communities can benefit from improved infrastructure on the main corridors in their neighborhood.

In particular, there are two Team Huizar sponsored visioning projects in CD14 that these funds will help move forward. The first is the "Rock the Boulevard" project in Eagle Rock. This community driven plan will include improvements such as a new traffic light, new crosswalks, curb extensions, pedestrian lighting, bus stop islands and protected bike lanes. These improvements will make Eagle Rock Boulevard a safer street for pedestrians, bicyclists and cars.

The El Sereno neighborhood will also benefit from this funding with increased safety based on the "Envision Eastern" project. This visioning plan will have numerous pedestrian improvements, including corner curb extensions, newly marked and highly visible crosswalks, perpendicular ADA compliant curb cuts at all crossings, curb extensions, and median islands. Sidewalk improvements on Eastern Avenue will include replacing trees that have uplifted sidewalks, making sidewalks ADA compliant, and infill street trees where they are missing. Repaving, restriping, speed feedback signs, new traffic signals and new stop signs will be installed to help with speed reduction efforts.

The total Metro funding will help complete these projects and many other future projects throughout North East Los Angeles and El Sereno. I am committed to working to expedite the funding so that the neighborhoods can benefit from the safety improvements.

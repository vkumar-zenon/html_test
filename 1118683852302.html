AUDITIONS, CASTING & ASSIGNMENTS

As many of you requested at the cookie festivities earlier this month, I would like to make our casting process as crystal clear as I possibly can.

We do adhere to this casting policy:
Northeastern students of any major and year of study are invited to audition for theatre department productions. Students are cast based on the actor's skill, experience, and qualities suitable for a given role regardless of ethnicity, race, gender, or national origin. All department casting follows university guidelines pertaining to equal opportunity and supports the Actor's Equity Association vision to encourage more equitable and authentic representation onstage by promoting diversity, gender parity, and inclusion.

It is vital to understand that, while production assignments are guaranteed by virtue of the curriculum, casting in our productions is not guaranteed.  Directors (especially guest directors) must be able to cast whomever they consider the best person for any given role. As faculty and staff, it is our goal to be extremely mindful about equitable representation and opportunities in our casting, to the best of our ability, given the composition of our department.

The faculty and staff do keep a list to help us keep track of who has been cast, who hasn't, and who is ready for the management, design and production assignments needed for our productions.  Every semester, after callbacks are concluded, the directors meet to negotiate the casting in the most careful way possible. When we have guest directors, a member of the faculty and/or staff joins them. After casting decisions are made, Marti, the Production Manager, in consultation with faculty and staff, begins the process of production assignments.

Please be assured that we take this process extremely seriously, and often take a very long time to accomplish it. Nonetheless, I can guarantee you that, even though we attempt to be as mindful as possible, there will always be some level of upset. I strongly urge you to discuss any concerns you may have with me directly, in order to avoid any unnecessary unpleasantness.

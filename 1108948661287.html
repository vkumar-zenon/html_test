Real Pickles Investor Update
October 2019
Happy Co-op Month, Real Pickles Investors!

Dill Pickles in the dill patch
at Atlas Farm
This October, we are crossing our fingers that the harvest season continues to be as good as it has. What a relief from last season and ALL the rain... So far, we've received all of our cucumbers and all of our Napa cabbage. Phew!  Now over 112,000 lbs of cabbage, 17,000 lbs of beets, and 16,900 lbs of carrots left to go (among a few other things). We've been able to source everything this year from our 8 core farms, except for a few small batch veggies and fresh herbs that we sourced from Vermont farms (see Dilly Beans below).   
 
There's a lot going on at Real Pickles these days, we hope you enjoy this update! 

Numbers
We sold $737,869 in Real Pickles products over the past 6 months (quarters 1+2 of our current fiscal year), up about 9% year-over-year and right on track with our projections.  While natural food stores (co-ops, independents, Whole Foods) remain our core customers, we've been branching out lately into more mainstream markets: 38 Big Y stores started carrying us in 2019, and 5 Roche Brothers stores and 1 Star Market in the Boston area added a selection of Real Pickles products to their shelves this fall.  Overall, our Kimchi, Beets, and Ginger Carrots are in big demand these days with sales of each up 25% over last year, while Turmeric Kraut sales have cooled off lately.
 
Plant-based Collaboration
We're excited to announce a new collaboration with Le Botaniste , a small chain of plant-based organic food and wine bars in New York City.  They approached us for help in making organic ferments for their salads, and we are thrilled to be supplying them with organic and regionally-sourced fermented vegetables. This is a high-volume food service customer for us and we're excited to learn more about similar opportunities as we expand our food service offerings.

Small Batch Dilly Beans!
Our newest small batch item for this year is a classic New England favorite. These beans are tender, crisp, and packed with fresh dill and garlic. Perfect for a mid-morning snack, packed in lunches, garnishing a happy hour cocktail, or chopped into a dinner salad.  We can't stop eating them, and we think you'll love these dillies all day long!

We sourced the beans from Deep Meadow Farm on the banks of the Connecticut River in Ascutney, Vermont. We appreciate that they are committed to caring for both land and community, "As stewards of the land we grow food with an important focus on the health of the land as we support the health of our community."
Look for Dilly Beans this winter! >>

Still life with pickles
This fall we worked with Brattleboro-based Clare Barboza to photograph our products in a kitchen setting with fresh veggies and in recipes.  Clare and her assistant Gretchen artfully created delicious-looking scenes to highlight our food and mission.  We're so happy to have connected with them and look forward to sharing these photos.

Newest worker-owner
We're so happy to welcome Jess Howard to our group of worker-owners. Jess has been with us for almost 3 years and has become an integral part of our team. They've worked with the production team, plus bookkeeping, marketing, shipping, and events. We look forward to birthdays and anniversaries here at Real Pickles, because Jess loves to cook and often brings in yummy homemade treats to share!  Jess lives in Greenfield with their partner and 2 cats (Garak and Chapel), and likes to read, collage, and play co-operative board games. Welcome, Jess!

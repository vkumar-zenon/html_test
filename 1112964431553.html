Cookies, Candles and Coconuts.  That's what I wanted to call it. I was overruled.  Read below to see what the new name is called. 

As we prepare for Christmas, take a moment for yourself. Reflect on your life and what you are doing and how. You may be happily surprised at what you find. Or, you may find yourself lacking in some way. That's how it is for me sometimes. I think it's called the human condition.

Crazy thing about Christmas is the Christian idea that Christ took on the human condition. We give it a fancy name: Incarnation.

"The doctrine of Incarnation says the eternal second person of the Trinity became a human being and "assumed flesh" in Jesus of Nazareth. Jesus Christ was the "Word made flesh" (John 1:14). The doctrine holds that Jesus was one divine person with both a divine and a human nature." So says my friend, Professor Don McKim, in page 161 of his fantastic book,  The Westminster Dictionary of Theological Terms, Second Edition." Hey, there's a gift idea.

St. John's Celebrates Advent

Each Sunday during Advent someone will light an Advent Candle on the Advent Wreath to begin our worship service.

We will enjoy an Advent Brunch and Crafts after worship on Sunday, December 8.

On the Sunday afternoon before Christmas, on  December 22, we have a new event called Carols, Cookies, and Cheer featuring carols on guitar and light refreshments.

The Season of Advent at St. John's will reach a climactic conclusion during our Candlelight and Communion Christmas Eve Service. 

Beyond all these activities, there is a deeper, inner meaning to Advent.

Advent, similar to Lent, is a season for self reflection and repentance. Thus, during Advent we often sing the seemingly dreary Advent hymns as opposed to cheery songs such as "Santa Claus Is Coming To Town"  because the one we anticipate during Advent, the King of Kings, born of Mary, coming again in power, is far from a cosmic Santa Claus. 

The Christ of Advent comes at first as a suffering servant. He comes again as the Judge of all creation. The power of Presbyterian theology is the understanding that our Judge, Christ, is our Redeemer. Because of this, we have an unfair advantage when it comes to our life evaluation.

7 Day Prayer Prompts

Opening prayer each day: Living God, we give you thanks and praise for the new things you are doing among us ...

Sunday ... for the transforming presence of Christ in Spirit-filled worship

Monday ... for your call to follow the way of Jesus in Lifelong Discipleship

Tuesday ... for the gift of your Word made flesh in Outward incarnations focus

Wednesday ... for the good news of your holy realm that we share in intentional authentic evangelism

Thursday ... for Jesus' example of humble service that we experience in servant-leader development

Friday ... for your great love for all the world that we show though caring relationships

Saturday ... for our common ministry in Jesus' name and for ecclesial health and vitality

Other joys and thanksgivings may be added ...

Closing prayer each day: Living God, in the great mystery of our faith receive us each night and revive us each day, that we may be dead to sin and alive to you;  through Jesus Christ our Savior. Amen.

What's going on at Defenders of Freedom?

Defenders of Freedom  connects the community to Veterans 
and provides donors with the organization they need 
to support Post 9/11 Veterans.
Lives Are Being Changed

One of the best things I can hear from a Veteran is: 
"I have hope again."
 
I have believed for years that helping a struggling Veteran by paying rent, keeping utilities on or putting food on their table when they are in crisis does more than just pay their bill.  We have given them hope.  Hope lessens despair which allows them to begin to see clearly again.  You have given them a chance to push forward.
 
In a call today with Leon, I heard those same words.   He told me, "Since I met you five months ago, I have gotten more done than in the past seven years. I have hope again."  I told him I was so glad he pushed forward and was still here.
 
He shared with me that he did try to end it all back in 2017. He had been fighting the VA for five years and had a small disability rating of 10%.  While the VA wasn't listening to him, his life was a wreck.
  • Dropped out of school numerous times because of post-traumatic stress
  • Charged for his GI Bill because he missed school
  • Was not able to hold meaningful employment
  • Became reclusive
  • He also has physical issues that he couldn't get addressed at the VA, complicating his depression.
Because of your generous support, we were able to help Leon through his financial crisis.  You helped save his life! Your support is critical to our mission.
 
Leon has sought different therapies through partnering organizations and he utilizes Eric Bourquin, our Veteran representative, for guidance on benefits and morale support.  It has been a real team effort.   THANK YOU!
 
While Leon has made progress, he still struggles on most days.  He needs surgery and brain treatment for his traumatic brain injury and post-traumatic stress.  His disability rating was increased to 90%, but he still hasn't started work.  Right now he is focusing on his mental health.
 
We MUST address the unseen injuries that many Veterans face. It is believed over 370,000 post-9/11 Veterans suffer from undiagnosed Traumatic Brain Injury. It is, perhaps, the biggest challenge facing these men and women and very little is being done to effectively treat them. Defenders of Freedom is committed to dramatically increasing our support to these men and women. We believe this is helping to curb Veteran suicide. I know you care about our Veterans just as much as I do. I know the work we do together for them is important to you.
 
Will you join me on Giving Tuesday, December 3rd, in our commitment to provide treatment to more Veterans with Traumatic Brain Injury?
 
Will you make a donation today that will give a Veteran like Leon his life back? I don't think there is a more generous thing we can do for them.
 
Thank you,

Donna Cranston Founder

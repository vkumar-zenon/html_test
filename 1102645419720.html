Asheville church re-images community on its property 
 
written by Connie Larkman
UCC Editorial Communications and News Strategist 

A village of micro homes is going up on the property of a planted United Church of Christ congregation in Asheville, N.C. It's part of a collaborative effort to share resources with those who share the same values, capping an ambitious to-do list for a new church started just 10 years ago.
 
"We are unapologetically followers of Jesus. We have a very expansive understanding of God. We created something out of nothing in response to the things in the church that didn't work for us and evolved out of a geographical grounding of our region," said the Rev. Sara Wilcox, pastor of Land of the Sky UCC.
 
But   Land of the Sky UCC  is not your typical faith community. It is entwined with a number of like-minded partners, both on-site and in surrounding Western North Carolina. The building in which it was planted - which it left for a time, then returned to and purchased - sits on just over four acres. Land of the Sky intends to use all of it for the betterment of life, and for transformative justice.
The micro homes are the latest step in a journey that has included multiple worship locations, creative stewardship of spaces, partnerships with church and community partners, and big visions for ministry with and to people on the margins.
 
'Space to do things collaboratively'
 
In 2009, the new church was planted at 15 Overbrook Place, nesting in what was Westminster Presbyterian Church. Three years later, Land of the Sky moved to another church, where it stayed for three more years. When Westminster Church planned to close, Land of the Sky reached out to the Presbytery of Western North Carolina and bought the building with the PWNC's support, to continue to use the campus for ministry. With the Presbytery as lender, the congregation is four years into an $800,000, no-interest purchase agreement.
 
"We call the property 15 Overbrook Place because we believe that the building will require collaborative space to sustain and maintain. We also believe the church is not a building," Wilcox said. It now houses two churches, Land of the Sky and its sister congregation, Circle of Mercy, formed in 2001 and affiliated with the UCC and Alliance of Baptists. The older congregation, Wilcox said, was very supportive when Land of the Sky began. Land of the Sky worships Sunday morning, Circle of Mercy worships in the evening.
 
The two congregations also share a number of multi-purpose spaces at 15 Overbrook Place with a business networking group, an improv group, a tai chi group and an aftercare program, and soon, a school.

General Synod Committees seeks feedback on Resolution process 
 
submitted by Rev. Carla Gregg-Kearns 
 member of the national UCC Board of Directors (Class of 2021) 
  
The United Church of Christ Board Governance Committee Subcommittee on General Synod Standing Rules and the Ministry Committee are requesting feedback from all, not just General Synod delegates.  Looking toward the next General Synod, these committees are discerning what new ways of experiencing the ministry of General Synod may look like, and are interested in thoughts on the General Synod resolution process, its place as a vital part of the ministry of the General Synod, and whether changes need to be made to the efficiency of the resolution process.  This survey was sent to all GS delegates, but planners are seeking a broader response, including churches and other stakeholders.  The deadline is January 10, 2020.  It will also be circulated on the ucc.org website for others to complete. 
   
Here is the survey:  https://www.surveymonkey.com/r/GSStandingRules

2020 Lightbourne Scholarship application procedure announced 
   
The Church Women of the Southern Conference provide scholarships to high school graduates and college students each year.

The deadline for applications for the James H. Lightbourne, Jr. Scholarship Fund for the upcoming 2020-2021 school year is March 1, 2020.

The James H. Lightbourne, Jr. Scholarship was established to assist needy and deserving high school graduates and college students who are members of the United Church of Christ in the Southern Conference to achieve their educational goals. Usually three (3) scholarships of $400 are awarded to each association. The scholarships will be sent from our treasurer directly to the colleges/universities chosen by the students.  The deadline for applications is March 1st of each year.   

If you are, or have, a high school senior graduating this year or a college student in your church that could possibly benefit from this fund, please encourage them to use the information found on the attached Dr. James H. Lightbourne, Jr. Applications and Guidelines to fill out the attached Application Form.

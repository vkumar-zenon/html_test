Holiday Shopping: What Small Business Need to know
5 Ways to Jumpstart your small business
Anthem, AZ - November, 2019 November is the time when small business owners start to prepare for the local shoppers, but don't just rely on your store fronts to boost your company visibility. With the likes of online shopping, small businesses can suffer unless you take the extra steps to create interactive shopping experiences.

Here are five ways that help boost your company sales during the Holidays.

1) Don't wait for "Black Friday" - make every Friday in November a Black Friday..... Create Special Offers each week and get creative.
  • Buy 2, Get 1 Free
  • Refer a friend, get a $20 credit
  • Collaborate with your retail center colleagues and create your own shopping experience on set day(s) >Twice a week

2) Take Advantage of Social Media - Local business owners should always be capitalizing on your company Facebook, Twitter, Instagram and other social media pages. Gaining social media engagement is powerful for small business. Generating fun and creative unique design ads or video clips will immediately engage your consumers. You can add a retail shop right to your Facebook or Instagram pages **NOTE - just because you may not have a product to sell, you can still promote your services in the same way.
3) Co-Promote with other Respectable Retailers in the Neighborhood
If you have a store front and/or have great business relationships with other
business owners in the area, reach out to them and collaborate. While they may be offering sales and discounts in their own shop space, it would be great if they could refer your business products/services too.
EX: CREATE A FLYER -
  • List all the businesses that want to participate and that are offering specials.
  • Print enough to have at each location.
  • Pass at the flyers to every customer and point out all the other specials that are being offered in the community for the holidays.
(I am pretty sure someone knows a printer that would like to get involved to help - After all, they have shipping services around the holidays too - Get them added to the list)
"Collaboration divides the task and multiplies the success"
4) Get Festive & Send out Invitations
You don't have to throw a party to send out invites, you just have to have a reason for your consumers to stop into your shop. Get Festive and decorate your store front (You can easily find dollar stores in the area to save $$)
EX: Use Social Media - "Hey everyone - TODAY ONLY, the first 25 people into our store today will receive a holiday gift or discounts towards their holiday shopping"

A Season of Gratitude

It is around this time of year that small businesses start to prepare for their Holiday Sales and special discounts, but is also a time of giving back. We can all be thankful for the blessings we are given throughout the year but now, you can also celebrate a Season of Gratitude by making choices in your life that leads you to the power of wisdom, joy and happiness.

We are getting close to end the decade! So how do you want to end your final months of the decade? When you surround yourself with positive people and influences, the world is more rewarding.

1) Show Gratitude to those that have helped or supported you. Send you personal hand written note cards or holiday cards. *shop local, perhaps purchase some gift certificates from local businesses and include them in your cards.
2) Set some time aside to volunteer or give back to a local charity (St. Vincent de Paul at St. Rose is currently looking for holiday foods for families)
3) Foster new business relationships who have a positive attitude towards people and life. Get rid of toxic people in your personal and professional lives.
4) Pamper Yourself - it is OK to treat yourself to a spa day or a day outside by the fireplace to take in a good book. Self-reflection often gets lost when dealing with the chaos of your everyday lives.
5) Be your BEST self. Do not compare yourself to others. When you believe in yourself, others will believe in you too. Living a healthy lifestyle can often contribute to overall happiness and confidence.

I hope you all have a wonderful November and wish you all the best of success during the holiday shopping months. If there is anything that I can help you with, please do not hesitate to give me a call.

Facebook Strategy:
Targeting your Audience

What does "Targeting the right audience" really mean? The word targeting is very controversial topic in the world of Facebook ads because there are many layers you must choose to reach a specific audience. Some believe it will increase the cost of your paid ad campaigns, while others may believe it will be a wise return of investment if executed correctly. A major component is understanding how artificial intelligence (AI) works. Planning your marketing strategy depends on a few major details that can help design successful marketing campaigns to promote your business products and/or services.

1) Who already has my ideal clients? In other words, your clients are already on Facebook so you will need to identify who they would be following already? If you own a fitness, health or lifestyle business then your ideal customers would already been following some of major brands and/or shopping at stores that are similar.
2) What do your customers or prospects already buy? If you can understand purchasing behaviors and patterns, then you will be able to identify the best way to engage your customers and campaigns will resonate better when you introduce new offers.
3) Where do your potential shoppers go? For example, if you wanted to pick up a new set of customers, where do they shop? When creating your campaigns, you can create key interests words (see below image) or hashtags that will be artificially recognized through Facebook algorithms. If someone just posted on Facebook, they received a smokin' deal on Amazon, then targeting Amazon shoppers would be a great way to target your audience if they bought similar products.

In short, find the similarities in your answers and then focus on that target audience. The only thing left is deciding on how much money you want to spend on your budget for each campaign. Establishing a marketing budget is so instrumental in the growth of your business. (featured in the December issue)

Don't worry, if the campaign is not working for you, the best thing about Facebook is that you can stop your running ads and you will not be charged for the whole campaign, only what was promoted up until the day you stopped it.

Breakfast and Study
"Human Nature and the Jewish Imperative: Standing Idly By or Getting Involved, a question of conscience?"

Rabbi Dr. Lee Bycel is a social justice advocate deeply committed to humanitarian issues. He currently serves as the Sinton Visiting Professor at the University of San Francisco. The focus of his teaching and research is Holocaust and Genocide, and Refugees and Immigrants. He has made several trips to Africa for humanitarian efforts. His book Refugees in America: Stories of Courage, Resilience and Hope was recently published. He is also a Senior Moderator at the Aspen Institute. For fifteen years Rabbi Bycel served as Dean of the Hebrew Union College - Jewish Institute of Religion in Los Angeles and is the rabbi emeritus of Congregation Beth Shalom in Napa. To learn more about his work and his recently published book - refugeesinamerica.com .

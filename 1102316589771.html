The Lighter Side...


Wisdom from the Indians

I've always loved the story of the cowboy who was riding along and came upon an Indian lying flat on the ground with his ear pressed to the earth. The Indian said, 

"Wait. A wagon. Two miles off. Drawn by two horses. One black. The other gray. Four people on board, man in a red flannel shirt, his wife, and two kids."

The cowboy was very impressed. He said, 

"It's amazing you can tell all that just by listening to the earth."

The Indian said,

"No, they ran over me thirty minutes ago, Go after them!"



Having Victory 
Over Violation Thinking

We are back at another annual church conference with our four friends around the Starbuck's table talking about what has happened at our churches during the last year. A year ago, one of the brothers had mentioned that their church was going to take their men's softball team out of the church league and have them compete in the city league. While breaking his plastic knife buttering his bagel, he was asked how that had worked out. He responded that three unchurched men had come to Christ and had been baptized. To this news came these questions from the violation thinker at the table, "How long do you require a person to wait after salvation before you allow them to be baptized? What course do you put them through so they are truly ready?" The ensuing discussion and debate caused the four friends to miss both the prayer time and the first session of the conference! The violation thinker was far more concerned about whether the new believers might not "totally" qualify for baptism than he was excited about the fact that they were saved and wanted to follow God. He was missing the blessing.

How do we defeat violation thinking?
The following are some suggestions to help us become less obsessed with what might go wrong in ministry and more fully enjoy the fruit of God's work in changing lives. 

- Guard your Mind
The Apostle Paul exhorted the Philippian believers to be alert about how they were thinking. Instead of looking on the dark side of their situations, they were to allow the peace of God to control their minds. In Phil.4:8-9 a formula of positive thinking is prescribed. To defeat violation thinking we will need to allow God and Scripture to "renew our minds". In Romans 12:2 we are admonished to not be like the pattern of the world..."fearful", but to be transformed by the renewing of our mind...then we will "be able to test and approve what God's will is," rather than be frozen into inaction by a fear of violating some man-made tradition or practice.

- Focus on Goals and Results
When we are excitedly pursuing God's purposes and plan for our life, we have momentum to see even more blessings flow from God in our ministries. Paul models and teaches this principle in I Corinthians 9:18-38 in vivid terms. Paul took risks and lived in a daring and disciplined way in order to achieve God's goals for him in reaching lost people. His actions and attitudes irritated the Pharisees of Judaism and some church members as well, but God gave him huge results. When we are busy being used of God to achieve His goals, it is more difficult to be distracted by violation thinking.

- Recognize Violation Thinking as Fear
Sometimes we fail to challenge the logic of violation thinkers because we don't want to oppose someone's "spiritual" sounding cautions. They seem so sincere and strong in their protest that we don't realize that it is actually a demonstration of fear. It is true that we are to fear God. But we are not to fear circumstances or the forcefully stated human preferences of others. Romans 8:15 states, "You did not receive a spirit of fear, but you received the spirit of Sonship..." And in another passage, "We fear not what man may do to us..." (or say to us or think about us.)

- Believe and Promote, Rather than Doubt and Defend
True faith in God recognizes what God is doing and seeks to join Him in that activity instead of holding back in fear and doubt. There is an air of excited anticipation and expectation, rather than a collecting of potential problems to inject into the thinking of those around us so they will not act either. "Great Commission Thinking" is characterized by believing and discipling others to step out and trust God.

- Celebrate Celebration
One of the best ways to defeat violation thinking is to continuously rejoice in the realizations of what God is doing in bringing lost people to salvation and new birth living. In Luke chapter 15, Jesus provided three parables concerning "the lost being found." A lost coin, a sheep, and a son are all found by someone intently searching for them. The very instructive truth in each case is how much celebrating was done when each was found. (Even the angels in heaven celebrated!) God's focus was on celebrating His grace in lost people being found. In these parables, only the "faithful son" in Luke 15:28-32 was concerned about fairness and a selfish focus on "justice" (violation thinking). God was all about finding and forgiving. We should be the same!

So, we should ask ourselves these questions,
- What's on my mind?
- Am I spending my time on what God is doing in changing lives?
- Or am I finding my mind focusing on who might be upset or what man-made rules might be broken?
We have a choice - violation thinking or living and celebrating in the power and practice of changed lives. The choice should be obvious...God has not given us a spirit of fear, but of power and of love and of a sound mind - II Timothy 1:7.

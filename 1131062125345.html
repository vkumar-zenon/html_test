ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT UPDATE
Illegally abstracting hertz revenue - USAID lowers losses.
Throughout 2019, to strengthen democratic institutions, deepen economic reform, and help foster conditions for broad-based, sustainable, and inclusive economic growth, USAID assisted the Albanian Electric Power Distribution Operator (OSHEE) to reduce electricity losses caused by illegal abstraction in the region of Shkodra. Through its Development of Regional Energy Markets (D-REM) project, it targeted the region with the highest aggregate, technical and commercial (AT&C) losses - around 35% of Shkodra customers recorded no electricity consumption. D-REM advanced a number of initiatives aimed at reducing AT&C losses, including introducing a loss control and recovery department, implementing a performance monitoring process for all meter readers, supervisors and regional management, and relocating and rehabilitating customer metering infrastructure.

As a result, D-REM reports that over 14GWh more electricity was billed to a total of about 8,500 customers. These bills will add approximately $2 million dollars to OSHEE revenue. Additionally, there were 13,500 inspections, 200 reports of theft, and 4,500 meter points were relocated to exterior points (making them accessible to OSHEE employees for reading and inspection). Consequently, the Government of Albania has requested that USAID expand its support to two other areas ( DurrÃ«s and Vlora). This would result in assistance covering over 30% of the Albanian electricity sector.

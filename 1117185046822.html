Enbridge in Minnesota - Thursday, December 12, 2019
LET YOUR VOICE BE HEARD - Tell the MN Public Utilities Commission to Approve Line 3's EIS
On Monday, December 9, 2019, the Minnesota Public Utilities Commission released the Line 3 Replacement Project's Revised Final Environmental Impact Statement, which confirmed that in the unlikely case of a spill on Line 3 there is no serious threat to Lake Superior. The PUC has opened a public comment period until JANUARY 16, 2020, seeking input on three topics:
  • Is the revised EIS adequate?
  • In light of the revised FEIS, what action should the Commission take on the application for a certificate of need for the Line 3 Replacement Project?
  • In light of the revised FEIS, what action should the Commission take on the application for a pipeline routing permit for the Line 3 Replacement Project?
Join other project supporters to make your comments in-person in Duluth on Thursday, December 19, 2019. An administrative law judge will take oral comments during two public hearings in Duluth.  This is solely an opportunity to comment on the questions listed above and is not a public meeting or public hearing for the purpose of providing information or answering questions. A transcript of the comments will be made by a court reporter and will become part of the record before the Commission. We are encouraging supporters to arrive early to make sure you get a seat in the room. 

DATE: THURSDAY, DECEMBER 19, 2019
TIME: Session 1 (1:00 p.m. - 4:00 p.m.) Session 2 (6:00 p.m. - 9:00 p.m.)
LOCATION: RADISSON HOTEL DULUTH - HARBORVIEW 
                     505 WEST SUPERIOR STREET
                     DULUTH, MN 55802

To Submit Written Comments: Visit www.mn.gov/puc select the Comment on the main menu to connect to the public comment page. Please include the Docket Numbers 14-916 and/or 15-137 in all communications. Comments received after the close of the comment period may or may not be considered by the Commission.  Comment Period closes January 16, 2020 at 4:30 p.m.

Line 3's EIS is already an in-depth 13,500 page environmental study of the project.  After four years of regulatory and permitting review, the replacement of Enbridge's Line 3 is the most studied pipeline project in Minnesota history. It is a $2.6 billion private investment in the state's critical energy infrastructure.

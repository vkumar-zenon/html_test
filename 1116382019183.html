Dear Parents,

Your children were so creative and hardworking this week as we got in the holiday spirit with winter crafts. Please feel free to stop by our hallway and classroom this upcoming week to see their beautiful work.

In chapel this week, we talked about how we can be a light in the world with our actions. We read the book Of Thee I Sing by Barrack Obama, which is based upon a letter he wrote to his daughters. It shares the story of many great people who used their special gifts to shine a light in the world and reminds us of all the good qualities we carry inside of us that we can use to make the world better, too.

On Monday, teachers were treated to a delicious luncheon provided by the Parent Organization. I am so appreciative of the parents who volunteered in the classroom on Monday for Gift of Time, so I could attend the luncheon. Thank you to Ted Hampton, Lauren Stokes, Karen Haley, and Jen Money-Brady for teaching and watching the class. Thank you also to the Parent Organization for the yummy lunch. It is wonderful to be part of such a giving community!

On Wednesday morning, we enjoyed Cocoa and Carols with the entire school. We gathered in the Lemler Garden around the Christmas tree to sing carols. We especially enjoyed warming up with hot cocoa following our singing.

In classroom community building, we discussed what bullying means. We discussed that bullying is different than annoying or mean behavior. Bullying is unwanted by one of the parties, aggressive (emotionally, verbally, or physically), repetitive (happens over and over), and involves power over another person. We also identified the types of bullying: verbal, physical, relational, and cyber. We talked and used activities to give students a collective frame of reference on the topic. Students worked in groups to sort pictures that showed different types of bullying and created posters that are hanging in the Grade 3/4 hallway.

We look forward to seeing you at the service of Lessons and Carols next week. Happy holidays and enjoy the vacation!

Best,
Miss Graber
Grade 3 Teacher

LOVING LEARNING:

In language arts this week, students continued reading and reflecting in guided reading groups, as well as researching and writing their early American research report. Students also had fun creating directed drawings of the Grinch that they painted in watercolor on Monday. Then, they wrote about how they would make the Grinch grin. Additionally, we completed winter haiku poetry.

This week in spelling, we focused on words with the soft-g spelling. We took our test over Unit 17 and pretest over Unit 19 on Friday. Please check your child's binder to see if your child should study the regular or alternate list for after we return from winter break.

In number corner, students made predictions about the the whole based on the unit fraction card uncovered. They also made equations to equal a specific fraction (3/4, 2/6, etc.)

In other math lessons, we completed problem strings related to rounding. We also practiced our estimation skills. We also dove back into work with addition of larger number in the context of story problems. We also reviewed subtraction on a number line, rounding and estimating to the tens and hundreds, and the students played the Round and Add to Hundreds game in partners.

In social studies,Lucy, Wilson, and Elaine did a wonderful job presenting their current events!

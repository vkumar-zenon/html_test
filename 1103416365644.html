Message From Gabe
As this year draws to a close I want to thank you for your efforts throughout the year. Thank you for the dedication and commitment you have shown to the City and to our community. This year has been filled with challenges and victories, and with the support of the City Council, we have continued to make progress in maintaining services . Let's reflect on some of our accomplishments:

  • Thanks to everyoneâs hard work and support, we started the year by adopting a balanced budget. This is no small feat given the complexity of local government finances and the time and attention spent analyzing City-wide needs and expenses. This year we witnessed a significant improvement in workflow with the recruitment of 114 new staff members. We also implemented a new over-the-phone interpretation services capable of serving customers in over 198 languages. In addition, we established a trust to address our unfunded CalPERS liabilities in the coming years.

  • In terms of development, we entered into an exclusivity agreement with the San Jose Sharks and have begun exploring and researching the idea of expanding the Sports Park to include an indoor recreational facility. We also approved a large number of construction permits (924 to be exact), including approval of the First and Kelton Commercial Project, a.k.a. "the Patio," which will include a restaurant and brewery, and finished our wayfinding sign project. And we supported our downtown by initiating two downtown pilot programs: The Downtown Commercial Corridor Development Incentive Pilot, and The Downtown FaÃ§ade Improvement Pilot. 

  • More recently, we improved City-wide fire response times by 36 seconds through the addition of an Alternative Service Model for providing fire services. We also continued modernizing recreation services by kicking-off Civic Rec software and #ReCreateGilroy. And everyoneâs excited to hear that we completed Phase 2 of the First Street/State Highway 152 Repairs which has allowed Caltrans to begin Phase 3 which includes upgrades to curb ramps and a complete repaving of First Street between the 101 on-ramps and Santa Teresa Boulevard. We have more accomplishments to be thankful for but too little space to list them all, but check out our end of year slide show for more. 

  • This year we have also faced some significant challenges. Together, in the face of unspeakable tragedy, we came together as a team and served our community with compassion and professionalism. We all did our part. Thank you for helping in all your various capacities to take care of the many visitors, residents, vendors, and businesses throughout the incident. Through your efforts, you helped our community feel safe in uncertain times. Our first responders and those on the scene will always have our gratitude. 

We have a very special team here. You are engaged and motivated and I am inspired by your passion for the community. Your excitement, energy, and contribution make this a terrific place to work. Thank you for all that you have done for the City in 2019. Best wishes and happiness to you and your families as we all look forward to a successful new year,  Gilroy's Sesquicentennial year, 2020 .

Sincerely,

-Gabriel

Proprioceptive Balancing
for Stability & Flexibility
(aka Isometrics)
with  Gary Williams, LMT
and Rosa Rodriguez, LMT, MFA

Saturday & Sunday, March 7 & 8, 2020
10am - 6pm
The Swedish Institute
151 West 26th Street, 5th floor
New York City

Many professions including Ortho-Bionomy employ isometric and isotonic techniques with their clients. In this two day workshop, you will learn when and how to appropriately use these techniques in order to stimulate self corrective reflexes, to work with inefficient muscular patterns, to increase range of motion, to release soft tissue, and to encourage ease, comfort, and tone within your clients. In particular, the use of Reciprocal Inhibition will be taught because of its ease and effectiveness. Dress comfortably.

Prerequisite: Open to licensed massage therapists, acupuncturists, and certain other licensed healthcare professionals.
Credit: 14 NY state hrs.*, 14 FL state hrs., 16 SOBI hrs. 
*This class has been approved for NY state hrs. because of its course content.
Tuition: $325.
Required Materials: Bring a sheet.

Gary Williams, LMT, and Rosa Rodriguez, LMT, MFA both maintain private practices in Manhattan. Gary is a Registered Instructor of Ortho-Bionomy, was on the Massage Therapy faculty at the Swedish Institute, College of Health Sciences in New York City for 20 years. Rosa is a Registered Advanced Instructor of Ortho-Bionomy, is an ACE certified fitness instructor, and has a MFA in dance. She taught fitness and dance for 12 years at the Borough of Manhattan Community College, CUNY, and toured the world as a dancer and personal fitness trainer.

Visceral: The Pelvis    4 spaces left!
with Mike Miller, LMT

Saturday & Sunday, April 18 & 19, 2020
9am - 6:30pm 
The Balance Arts Center 
151 West 30th Street, 3rd floor
New York City

In this 19 hour class we will use Ortho-Bionomy to explore the urinary tract and the reproductive system. We will work with the individual organs and look at their relationship to each other. We  will also look at the surrounding soft tissues and the supporting structures. We will work with the kidneys, ureters, bladder, uterus, prostate, ovaries, spine, pelvis, and the pelvic floor. 

This workshop is based on the work of the late, Darlene Smith.  Come join us and learn how the viscera play a vital role in our health and well being. Dress comfortably.

Prerequisite:  Phase 5 required, Phase 6 recommended
Credit:  19 SOBI elective hrs., 17 NCBTMB CEU's

Mike Miller, LMT, is an Associate Advanced Instructor of Ortho-Bionomy. He spent 6 years assisting Darlene Smith in her Visceral workshops. Mike is a thoughtful, thorough instructor who is committed to helping people understand and practice the principles of Ortho-Bionomy in order to help their clients and themselves live better lives.

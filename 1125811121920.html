Ice Melt - Frequently Asked Questions

  • What surfaces are safe to use ice melt on?
  • All properly cured and sealed concrete over 1 year old as well as asphalt and gravel.

  • Is there a product that is safe to be used around pets?
  • Yes, most ice melters are safe and non-toxic to use around pets. There are products such as magnesium chloride and urea that are the safest options.

  • How do customers avoid/prevent damage to surfaces?
  • Use the product as directed on the bag/jug. Clean off all run-off or leftover product after the snow and ice are melted. Watch application rates (less is more). Find a product strong enough to work the first time you apply.

  • Are ice melters safe to use near vegetation?
  • Yes, but they should not be used directly on vegetation. When removing excess product, do not push onto vegetation.

  • How to ice melters work?
  • By breaking the bond between the snow or ice and the surface you wish to clear. Some ice melters require the temperature, friction, or the sun to help, while others create an exothermic reaction by pulling moisture from the air into itself to create heat.

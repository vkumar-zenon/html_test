Disability in New York State for owners
Are you aware, for a very small annual amount, some owners can be included in the NYS statutory disability policy? Many owners do not include themselves and can do so, depending on the insurance carrier. What this coverage does is give you up to 26 weeks of income. However, the minimum policy only pays $170 per week. It has paid that amount since 1990 that I'm aware of. For a minimal increase in premium many carriers offer you and your employees up to 5 times that ($850) plus more if you're in the hospital. No one can live on $170 per week. Coverage is only for 26 weeks yet it will help if you have any kind of surgery, have an accident and can not work.

Your tip of the day is call your agent and see what your NYS Disability payout is per week, and find out what it will cost to increase it. You will not be sorry. In my opinion, it's the cheapest coverage you can get and doesn't go up if you have a claim!!

P.S. If you deduct the .60 per week from your employee, only a fraction of what is paid out is taxable as well!

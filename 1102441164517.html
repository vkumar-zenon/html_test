For the first time on the Kohl Mansion stage,  we are featuring a magnificent Franz Rausch fortepiano made in 1841 in Vienna!

Bring family and friends to celebrate the season in the lavishly decorated Kohl Mansion at our beloved Holiday Gala Concert. Musicians from Sonoma's Valley of the Moon Music Festival will fill the Great Hall with festive sounds played on their magnificent period instruments. 
A Gala Champagne Reception follows. 

Chopin: Ballade No. 1 in G minor, Op. 23
Brahms:  Horn Trio in E-flat Major, Op. 40
Mendelssohn: Piano Trio No. 2 in C minor, Op. 66
Pre-concert Talk with Kai Christiansen at 6 pm.

Built right around the time the Chopin Ballade and Mendelssohn Trio were written, the superb Rausch fortepiano is straight-strung and has an all-wooden frame, unlike the modern piano with its steel frame and crossed strings. These features give it a quieter tone (though with a large range of expression), a deep resonant bass, and great clarity in all registers. On the front of the piano it can be seen that this model won a prize at the time, which we think was well merited!  The Kohl Mansion's celebrated acoustics will showcase this beautiful period instrument in all its splendor.

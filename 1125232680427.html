Question:  
Why is winter the best time to check for radon in my home or office?

Answer: 
January is designated National Radon Action Month by the EPA to raise awareness of radon as the 2nd leading cause of lung cancer. Radon levels usually test at their highest in winter when the house is all closed up and there is less outside ventilation. Additionally we spend more time indoors in winter, increasing our exposure to in-air contaminants. Levels can (and should) still be tested any time of the year, but winter readings tend to be a "worst case scenario". Radon Solutions of CT is the radon division of Water-Flo and the same team you trust for your water solutions can also address your radon concerns. You can find more information about radon and radon solutions on our website  Radon Systems page and in our  FAQs. Give Water-Flo a call to test your home for radon in air or water every 2 years (EPA recommendation) or if you've done construction in the lowest level of your home (e.g. finished your basement).

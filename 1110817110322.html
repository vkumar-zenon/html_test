Royal Canin is considered a super food and Cavaliers do not need as much as with other brands. All fosters on Royal Canin should only be feeding their dogs 1/3 a cup twice a day. You can also add 5 or so green beans (low sodium) to the top of the food as an extra treat. Always remember to keep treats to a minimum. Fall Threats
With fall being upon us and the holidays just around the corner, here are just a few things to be watchful for that could effect your foster's health:

  • Acorns: They are toxic! They can also cause a life-threatening bowel obstruction if swallowed whole. If you suspect your foster has eaten acorns, contact us ASAP.
  • Dough and Batter: Dough can rise in their stomachs and cause bloating and upset as well as contain raw eggs which can carry Salmonella
  • Turkey bones and skin: A little unseasoned turkey is okay for your foster, just no skin or cooked bones. And remember to keep the treats at a minimum! Cavis are prone to holiday weight, too!
  • Nutmeg: Too much nutmeg can be harmful, so no sharing the pumpkin pie!
  • Corn Cobs: Corn isn't necessarily bad for dogs, but the cob can be life threatening if eaten by causing a bowel obstruction.

As always, if your foster isn't feeling well (diarrhea, wheezing, etc.) or worse, have ingested something they shouldn't have, been bitten by a snake, suffered an injury, etc., contact Ashley ASAP. Don't hesitate. Call and text Ashley first and she will provide instructions from there. This may include an immediate visit to the vet, but always call and text Ashley before anything else. 
 
Happy fall everyone! Thank you again for all you do for our dogs and our program!

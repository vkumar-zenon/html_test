We are excited to announce that Clay England has accepted the position of Chief Human Resources Officer at Bay Area Hospital after serving as the interim since July 2019!

After a national search that included a number of onsite interviews of external candidates over the last month, Clay was selected for the permanent role. One of his attributes is his energy around workforce development as the hospital works to create an attractive environment for existing and future employees.

EHR Transition Updates
Bay Area Hospital and its partners Bay Clinic, North Bend Medical Center, and Advanced Health (collaboratively known as the South Coast Ecosystem), are planning to move to Epic as a shared electronic health record (EHR).

St. Charles Health System will partner through the Epic Community Connect program to provide the new platform. Epic will provide an integrated technology platform for clinicians, patients, and staff that will allow them to work collaboratively to improve patient care, innovate healthcare delivery, and achieve financial health.

A comprehensive selection process for an Epic partner began back in January of this year. The choice to move to Epic came about as a means to replace multiple EHRs with a new integrated system that will support care coordination, evidence-based care, standardization, and greater efficiency. This transition to Epic will enhance the patient safety, collaboration, and efficiency required by the South Coast Ecosystem in order to meet patientsâ unique needs across the continuum of care.

St. Charles was selected as a partner due to the fact that they provided a detailed proposal including staffing, governance, and support for all participants. In addition, St. Charles demonstrated a strong commitment to partnership throughout the due diligence process.
 
The next steps are to work collaboratively with St. Charles Health System to reach a definitive agreement. This work will begin in concert with operational readiness, team resource planning, integration, change management, and long term maintenance of the system.

Flu Activity Level Indicators
When the Center for Disease Control (CDC) reports geographically widespread influenza (flu) in Oregon, when there is local or regional high influenza-like illness (ILI), the use of a facemask will be mandatory for employees who work in or enter clinical patient care areas and are not immunized against seasonal influenza .
 
Once the face mask requirement has been implemented, it will be in effect for the duration that the criterion is present (that both geographically widespread influenza activity  and  high ILI activity exist in Oregon) and for 2 weeks after this criterion ceases.
 
The face mask is to be worn at all times when an employee enters a patient room, or when an employee is within 6 feet of patients in clinical patient care areas.
 
Employees who choose to get the influenza vaccine after the criterion is reached will be required to wear a face mask for two weeks following receiving the vaccine.

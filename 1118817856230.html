DVD Production of The Mod Mill is still available


 
The DVD Set is entitled "The Mod Mill Scrapbook" and includes 15 chapters with a total length of over 5 hours. The images have all been digitally enhanced and edited to look the best possible.

  Chapter 1 is a history of the old mill, a description of the Mod Mill club formation and operation, the taking and destruction of the building by The Pennsylvania Department of Highways and the efforts to relocate and keep the club alive.  This chapter is a 10-minute documentary and is narrated by me. The old photos give a visual tour of the many rooms in The Mill and the fun, music and the people that made the magic happen for all of us.

 Chapter 2 is an 8mm silent film shot in 1968/69 that will take you back to all the sights, the feeling, the dancing, and the dress.  The High Keys are on stage, and the membe rs dance in a circle to the song that had become our signature -  The Youngbloods -"Get Together".
 
 
Chapters 3 & 4 are slide shows that feature many members of the staff - cooks, waitresses, bartenders, and security. You will see a slideshow of many of the original entertainers who performed at The Mill.  Slides of the entertainment schedules as published in "The Choaf Scene" monthly newspaper end this chapter.  The soundtrack for this segment is a live recording of "The Limits" at The Mod Mill during their farewell performance in 1967.

Chapter 5 is a slideshow that is just a collection of photos of the People of The Mill.  (you may be in it !)  There are hundreds of faces in the photos -  many of our famous "Smoochie Game" and our Halloween Costume Parties.
 
                  
   
Chapters 6 - 15 cove r the Reunions held in 2009, 2010 and 2014.     These segments highlight many of the very talented young performers who played on the Mod Mill Stage and have kept that talent alive to this day.   

Reunion 2009 was part of The Musikfest Festival in Bethlehem - The Limits reunite and welcome Joe Colarusso and Steve Molchany to join them on stage.   

Reunion 2010 held at The Meadows in Hellertown headlined the amazing reuniting of The High Keys with Johnny Defrancesca, Joe Colarusso, Marty Druckman and Gino Schimpf.  Other headliners were The Limits, Fiasco and Paragon, the house band at The Meadows.

Reunion 2014 was also at The Meadows and this time, we filled both banquet rooms.  The Large Flowerheads performed in one room with full sets of their award winning dance music of the 60's. Two songs feature Tommy Zito on keyboards and vocals as a rare and special treat. Tommy was one of the three VIP Emcees along with Joe Colarusso and Freddie Frederick Sr. 
In the other room was non stop live music staring classic musicians, many who played at The Mill. It was a super Jam Session.  The DVD Set has 9 chapters of HD video opening with Gina Ross, backed by former members of The Dooley Invention.

The next chapter is "A Band Called Clyde" with a cameo of Nan GerHart. Chapter 13 features The Jim Young Jam with Tommy Crist, Dan Frederick, Joe Mayernik, and Skip Romagnoli. During the set, Jim Young's son Marc who is now also a successful musician,  joins his father for dual keyboards.
 A separate chapter includes a tribute to Jim's famous father "Ragtime Charlie Young,  who would play honky tonk piano and lead sing-a-longs at the Mod Mill's "Old Timers Nites" every few months. Charlie recorded at least four albums for RCA - one live at the mill. He would often play at the "Old Mill Inn" for Eddie Sachs, Indianapolis 500 race driver, who operated the mill's niteclub in the years before The Mod Mill.
 
 Rick Levy of The Limits phoned in during the evening and a chapter is devoted to his comments. The Limits have always been great supporters of The Mod Mill and had reunited for both previous reunions.

The Finale chapter of the 2014 Reunion is The Joe Colarusso Jam with Kevin Lang, June Thomas, Scott Ward, Wayne Maura and Bill Lance from the original "Jay & The Techniques".   Emmitt Harris also joins the Jam with a  tremendous performance of "Purple Rain".

Due to missing video footage from the 2014 Reunion,  four former members of The Dooley Invention staged a special "re-take" show at Pitcher's Lounge in Allentown.  The final chapter captures these classic musicians in the full 30-minute set that was performed at the reunion and features Richie Kerecz, Dave Wagner, Amedeo Borsetti and Wayne Achey.

This project grew to epic proportions.  A visual and audio keepsake that those who attended The Mod Mill, those who are interested in the history of the area and the extreme talent of our homegrown musicians, will certainly enjoy and want to show to family and friends.

Original Hand Made Mod Mill Tables are on sale
   
 
These unique tables were hand crafted from the woods surrounding Indiantown Gap Military Reservation near Harrisburg Pennsylvania in the 1930's Great Depression and World War II. They were made by The Youth Conservation Corp, a government social service program that helped the teenagers of unemployed and military families.
 
Each piece is unique and was constructed from tree branches and ammunition crates. The tables were used by famous Indianapolis race car driver Eddie Sachs when he operated the "Old Mill" in the 1950's and early 60's. They were part of the package of furnishings that was included in the rebirth of the landmark mill as The Mod Mill. They have been in dry barn storage ever since the destruction of the building in 1970. Some even have carvings of names and initials. Some have pieces missing, but most are in surprisingly good condition.
 
We have tried to research the validity of the story of the furniture's origin as told to us by Mrs. Sachs but have no definite proof.  Some of the table tops do have military markings on the back, and I have read articles that mention such busy-work projects the teens were assigned.
 
I need to make room in my barn and are selling these tables at reasonable prices. Contact me, Jerry@ModMill.com or just call anytime between 9 & 9 at 610-714-9025.  I will be setting up times to view them in person or by FaceTime calls.  I would love to have these tables in the homes of those folks who have emotional connections with "The Olde Mill" and "The Mod Mill" but I will be putting them up on eBay soon.

Thanks for the memories and your support through the years!
Jerry Deane

In the very first Venu Newsletter in October 2017 we provided an article explaining the Election Cycle at Venu and the importance of every homeowner voting. For this newsletter we have updated the article to reflect current events. 
 
Our bylaws require a mere 10% participation for a quorum; that is only 38 units out of 388. Our communityâs track record for participating in the election process has improved only slightly in the last five years. From 66 votes or 17% in 2014, votes increased to 99 ~26% in 2018.   We can do better!  
 
The goal of this article is to ensure that every homeowner is aware of the election process, understands the importance of their vote, and is empowered to participate. 
 
Why Participate?  
 
The Venu Board of Directors is currently comprised of five* members.â¯ These individuals will make every decision about the Venu for the coming year  be it financial, architectural, management, appearance, etc. For example: 
 
  • Should dues be increased? 
  • Should the annual budget or reserves be modified? 
  • What improvements/enhancements should be made this year? 
  • Should Rules & Regulations be amended? 
 
* Hot off the press:  The Venu Board voted to increase the Board of Directors from 5 to 7 members for the 2020 election to split the workload and accomplish more. 
 
There is no system of âchecks and balancesâ. We donât have a Senate and a House. There is no Parliament.  There is also currently not a term limit for a Venu Board member and some of our current Board members have served for more than five years.   Often voters choose an incumbent because it may be easier to vote for a known name than to get to know someone new. Venu needs a mix of the incumbent knowledge and fresh ideas that can come from people who have not participated before. Venu voters need to be able to make a choice and the more people that run the greater the choice. 
 
In a community of this size your vote matters.  Votes are based on units, not individuals .  An owner gets one vote for each unit they own. Venu has numerous owners of multiple units, some ten or more.  We need ten owners with one unit each to match the vote of one owner with ten units.   We need every homeowner to vote regardless of the number of units they own. â¯Every vote counts!

Roofing Project Update
The Venu Board of Directors formed a task force last spring to tackle the repair of roofs at Venu. Roofs were inspected in spring of 2019, and July brought the latest roofing repair project to its completion. We saw all 42 roofs: cleaned, broken tiles replaced, corner balls repaired, slipped tiles repositioned and debris removed. We hope you didnât experience any inconvenience while this work was done. 

Further funding for the replacement of our roof underlayment was discussed at both the September Architectural/Finance committee meetings and the Open Board Meeting held September 17th. The typical life expectancy of the roof underlayment is 30 years, of which 20 have passed. Our expectation is that the underlayment will have to be replaced within the next 10 years at a cost of $1,600,000. Reserves currently being collected will not meet this need in 10 years. 

Instead of implementing a onetime assessment, of over $4,000 per unit, there was a $3.00/sf/per unit increase proposed to our monthly HOA fees, to start in January 2020. The money collected would be earmarked solely for the use of the roof underlayment project. A detailed spread sheet was made available at the September 17th Board meeting for all to see how/why this proposal was determined. On September 18th this documentation was also emailed to all homeowners with an email on file or mailed to homeowners without an email on file. 

The final vote on this will be at the October 15th Board meeting. Homeowners are encouraged to attend in person or via the GoTo Meeting link, to share their thoughts and hear those of our Board of Directors.

Window Replacement Update
Many residents voiced concerns about leaky, drafty and inoperable windows as well as not being able to clean windows because the upper 3rd floor units are unable to be cleaned by a window cleaning company.  In early 2019, the Architectural Committee started the process of choosing window replacement options to be approved by the Venu Board and the Grayhawk Architectural Committee.  The Committee checked on many of the window replacement companies available to us. They took into consideration a plethora of reasons to go about finding the right window to choose.   

Factors of those choices include: 
  • A reputable company that stands behind their product with quality installers and distributors that includes a full LIFETIME One Time Transferable WARRANTY 
  • Tilt-in ability to wash from inside unit 
  • Best affordable value for our community 
  • Company that provides installation to a 3rd floor building 
  • Low frame profile for more glass pane view ability 
  • Provides all sizes of product needed for Venu units; including patio doors  
  • Acceptable color to match Venu building color schemes 

You may or may not have seen some of the choices that have been installed and removed through the summer, but it has been finally and fully determined that SIMONTON DAYLIGHTMAX WINDOWS is our choice.

In the coming weeks, you will receive information on how to go about the process of replacing your unitâs windows. There will be certain criteria you need to follow based on both VENU and Grayhawk Regulations. There will also be a window display in the Great Room once those regulations are determined.
   
ï»¿At this time it is an optional improvement for your unit, not a mandatory replacement. But once you see the windows being installed throughout the property and talk to the neighbors that have replaced their windows, we think you will see the benefits new windows can make to your unit.

Parking Changes and New Handicap Spaces
This past summer the Venu Board, on the recommendation of the Parking Task Force, enacted some changes to the parking spaces and regulations at Venu. 
 
The first change was to add more handicap parking spaces. The City of Scottsdale brought it to our attention that according to the cityâs new handicap parking code we were short and out of compliance. The new ordinance requires that four percent (4%) of parking spots be dedicated to handicap space, 30 spaces at Venu. The Board felt this was excessive for a private community and were able to negotiate with the city to âgrandfatherâ our community to the federal ordinance of two percent (2%), 15 spaces at Venu. Having only six (6) handicap spaces, we still lacked the correct number to be compliant. Nine âred spotsâ around the community were converted to handicap parking spaces bringing the community into compliance. 
 
All remaining âred spotsâ were converted to âpermitted parkingâ to alleviate driving around looking for a parking spot for those who have more cars than garage spots. These permitted parking spots are available on a first come first serve basis and are permitted on a minimum of three-month timeline. Reach out to the Concierge if there is a spot that you would like to reserve for $75/month. There are also a few garages available on a permit basis.  
 
Please be mindful of where you are parking. If the sign posted at the spot or painted on the curb says âRESERVEDâ or âPERMITTEDâ then DO NOT PARK in that spot, even if only for a few minutes. These spots belong to someone and you run the risk of having your car towed.  
 
Guest Parking is just that, for guests. These spots cannot be parked in from 11pm to 7am without being registered with the Concierge.

Winter Landscaping Tips
Just because winter is here does not mean it's time to let your landscape go. Learn how to add texture, color, and beauty to your yard with these six simple winter landscaping tips from Barbara Pierson and Better Homes & Gardens:

  • Focus on bark: Sure, deciduous trees lose their leaves in wintertime, leaving their branches and trunks in focus. This can be a good thing! Some interesting ornamental trees that have really distinctive bark will end up adding winter interest, Pierson says.
  • Include berries: Many trees and shrubs have berries they hold onto during fall and winter, and those can provide food for birds overwintering in your area.
  • Remember evergreens: Evergreens are the staple winter tree, and also make good focal points year-round.
  • Rely on your hardscape: The solution to your winter landscape might not be a plant at all. This is the best time to consider hardscape and the addition of a bench, trellis, or even a sculpture that can make all the difference.
  • Adorn your summertime containers: Window boxes, hanging baskets, etc. are indispensable for winter landscaping. Take advantage of these and fill them with evergreen boughs!
  • Stick with four-season perennials: Some perennials have evergreen foliage making them great for winter landscaping.

Winter is also a great time to stock up on the nonplant elements you'll need for next year's gardening and landscaping and the perfect time to research possible plants. If you have a vision for your spring landscaping, contact Serene Landscape Group and we'll help bring it to life!

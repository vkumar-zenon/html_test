Hello, it's been a while...

What a year of change! Maybe for you too. For me, for us, we are doing our best to settle in. Our big move to the Hudson Valley has been the news of 2019. We have not sent holiday cards in many years but wanted you to know how much we appreciate yours when they arrive. Life is so rich with love and light and hope and wonder. I offer some reflections from 2019...
Just Dance...
I danced with friends on my birthday last January and I plan on doing the same thing this year! When I dance, I change, I grow, I learn something more about myself. Freeing myself up ( Nia FreeDance ) has been a game-changer and I look forward to what more will emerge through this healing practice in the months to come.
My heart and my soul will always be called to live theatre. In the spring, I FINALLY saw "Hamilton." It really does deserve all the praise. In my humble opinion, it's a masterpiece. I don't make resolutions but at the top of my 2020 wishlist is more theatre. Who's with me?
Moving forward...
Something I learned ~ when I have a conversation with Darmon about how short life is and that if we need to put moving out of Maplewood on the table, Get Ready . It might actually happen.
Saying goodbye In August after 19 years in Maplewood was one of the most painful experiences and one of the most glorious sensations all at the same time. Loving with that much ferociousness and feeling how deep my connections run are worth every heart-ouch . I will forever treasure all of what I shared for so many years with my NJ friends, my created family.
New Beginnings Party!
What a gift to be honored so beautifully. The send off from my Nia NJ family was a highlight of 2019. Actually, it will be a shining light forever in my heart. Recently I was looking for a candle and I was so touched that some of the candles gifted to me had names like "Roots" and "Nest." Yes. I needed that about now. Thank you friends.
"Butterfly are free to fly."
Although the gift of a chrysalis was given to me in September of 2018, it is the gift that keeps on giving. It has gently called forth a rebirth of my spirit. My prayer is that I always remember how it felt to sit with her, that butterfly, as she emerged and prepared to fly.
"Be still, stand in love, pay attention."

Dwell In Possibilities Retreat
DIP 2019 was in early November, right after my dear friend's father's passing. ( And last year, we gathered right after my mom died .) Teresa and I came together and we held each other up, along with the incredible women who attended. We celebrated, mourned, danced, told stories, cried and laughed. Truth-telling, being playful and togetherness. What a blessing to guide and be guided. What a gift to listen and be listened to, to love and be loved.
Fingers crossed that it is only the flu...
My father and my sister had a trip planned in November to visit. My dad's heart had other plans. After swiftly getting him to the hospital (thank you, Tina), we found out it was not the flu. He needed a pacemaker. We were told his heart is strong, but that the electrical circuitry was not working properly. Look at his sweet face. And that was less than 24 hours after surgery! Life is so precious. We're so relieved he is ok and feeling stronger everyday. He was MOST upset that he missed a New York Voices gig!
So, that's 2019 in a nutshell. I have felt so anchored by dear friends who have given me so much room to feel all the feels with ALL this change in the air. I continue to love my work and my clients, and the 3-day a week commute into NYC isn't so bad. Looking ahead, I am hoping to open a small psychotherapy practice up here, maybe in the spring. I am getting my dancing feet wet with a Saturday class-share and have just peeked out to perhaps add another weekly class. My roots run deep in the NJ community, and returning every other month to offer classes and FreeDance experiences has felt so nourishing. Mostly, I have honored the liminal space I have been in. That space between where I've been and where I'm going, who I've been and who I'm becoming. Oh, what discoveries and "ah-ha's" I have found in this luscious space.

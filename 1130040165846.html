Transitional Care Management
Transitional care refers to the coordination and continuity of health care during a movement from one healthcare setting (hospital) to the primary care physician within 7 or 14 business days.  
Coding 99495 and 99496 takes more effort than deciding whether the patient is seen 7 vs. 14 days after discharge.                                                               
The Difference
CPT added Transitional Care Management service codes in 2013 to reward medical providers for care that facilitates the transition of a patient with moderate or high complexity medical decision-making (MDM) from an inpatient hospital setting to the patientâs community setting. These codes acknowledge a providerâs comprehensive responsibility and coordination of care for a patient, from discharge through 29-days after the date of discharge from inpatient status includes acute hospital, rehabilitation hospital, and long-term acute care hospital.                                                           
  • Transitional Care Management Services with the following required elements (CPT code: 99496)
  • Communication (direct contact, telephone, electronic) with the patient and/or caregiver within 2 business days of discharge.
  • Medical decision making of high complexity during the service period.
  • Face-to-face visit, within 7 calendar days of discharge-should include -patient here for post hospital/acute care/SNF, admit and discharge dates and medication reconciliation.

Quality Update
2020 Ambulatory Health Care National Patient Safety Goals Released by The Joint Commission. The purpose of the National Patient Safety Goals is to improve patient safety. The goals focus on problems in health care safety and how to solve them. Here are TWO goals for your review.

Two Patient Identifiers is a Joint Commission regulation that must be followed as part of our accreditation. You will find specific information on this topic located on the F:Drive in:
  • 2019 CFHC policies and procedures, Quality Manual, QI Forms, Jan 2018 National Patient Safety Goals, Goal 1, NPSG.01.01.01

         Goal 1- Improve the accuracy of patient identification. NPSG.01.01.01
  • Use at least two ways to identify patients. For example, use the patientâs name and date of birth. This is done to make sure that each patient gets the correct medicine and treatment.
  • Rationale for NPSG.01.01.01-Wrong-patient errors occur in virtually all stages of diagnosis and treatment. The intent for this goal is two-fold:
  1. first, to reliably identify the individual as the person for whom the service or treatment is intended;
  2. second, to match the service or treatment to that individual. Acceptable identifiers may be the individualâs name, an assigned identification number, telephone number, or other person-specific identifier.

         Goal 7 - Reduce the risk of health careâassociated infections. NPSG.07.01.01
  • Comply with either the current Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) hand hygiene guidelines or the current World Health Organization (WHO) hand hygiene guidelines.
  • Rationale for NPSG.07.01.01-According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, each year, millions of people acquire an infection while receiving care, treatment, or services in a health care organization. Consequently, health careâassociated infections (HAIs) are a patient safety issue affecting all types of health care organizations.
  1. One of the most important ways to address HAIs is by improving the hand hygiene of health care staff.
  2. Compliance with the World Health Organization (WHO) or Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) hand hygiene guidelines will reduce the transmission of infectious agents by staff to patients, thereby decreasing the incidence of HAIs.
  3. To ensure compliance with this National Patient Safety Goal, an organization should assess its compliance with the CDC and/or WHO guidelines through a comprehensive program that provides a hand hygiene policy, fosters a culture of hand hygiene, monitors compliance, and provides feedback.

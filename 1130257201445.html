Plan Sponsor Attitudes 
As an open architecture service provider, NWPS partners with some of the most respected money managers in the industry. Many of these "DCIO" providers offer white papers and studies that examine timely issues and trends in the industry. Fidelity Institutional Asset Management has released their tenth annual Plan Sponsor Attitudes Survey entitled "The Expanding Role of the Plan Advisor." This important survey contains invaluable insights for the retirement plan advisor. For example, did you know that: 

  • The #1 reason plan sponsors switched advisors is that they were seeking a more knowledgeable advisor?

  • The #1 reason for hiring an advisor is to understand how well the plan is working for employees and how to improve on that? 

  • The #1 response to what impact does an advisor have on the plan, was to help with fiduciary requirements? 

  • The #1 concern of plan sponsors is whether the plan is helping prepare participants financially for retirement? 

Indeed these rankings perfectly correlate with industry pundit Chis Carosa's recent article "The 5 Biggest Worries of 401k Plan Sponsors and What to do About Them." Clearly the role of the plan advisor is complex and multi-faceted and to simply be talking about funds or fees misses the mark. The Fidelity study is particularly insightful as it draws from over 1,200 respondents from plans of all sizes and from a cross section of decision makers including owners, financial professionals and human resources personnel. For the advisor team that is seriously looking to retain and grow their retirement plan business, the Fidelity study is a must-read. 

To obtain a copy, contact your Fidelity DCIO representative or just send me an email ( tobrien@nwp401k.com) with the words "Fidelity Study" in the subject line.  
The Business of Being in the Retirement Plan Business
Tim Wulfekuhle, President & CEO of NWPS was recently interviewed on the 401k Fridays podcast hosted by Rick Unser.  Rick and Tim dive into the topic of independent recordkeepers and their place in the retirement industry. What does it mean to be a truly independent recordkeeper (answer: no products or investments to sell) and how viable is that business model? 

One of the more interesting tangents was their discussion of participant solicitation. For those service providers with investments to sell, post termination participant rollovers is a necessary part of their revenue model. Indeed, many large, bundled product providers price their recordkeeping services in anticipation of rollovers. Of course, new fiduciary rules may prohibit encouraging participants to roll from an institutional share to a more profitable retail share. It's a fact: retirement plan fiduciaries now need to care about (worry about?) how providers promote non-plan related products and services. See: Loren L. Cassell, et al., Plaintiffs v. Vanderbilt University et al., Defendants.  The independent recordkeeper (well, at least NWPS) causes no such concerns. 

Tim also shares his thoughts on consolidation trends, bright shiny objects, capturing employer and employee attention when you don't have a household name, cybersecurity and what the future of the retirement business could look like. It's a broad, thoughtful conversation which will further your understanding of this complex and ever changing business.  Take a listen!  
Keeping Up: a Roundup of the Best Retirement Plan Information Sources    
We're all busy. Yet, as the previously mentioned Fidelity study notes, plan sponsors are seeking more knowledgeable plan advisors. How to keep up?  Below, we highlight some of the better (and free!) information sources, many of which will send you a daily or weekly email as to what's happening in the industry. 
 
The Broadcast Retirement Network - sign up for the daily BRN newsletter, The Morning Pulse, publishing timely retirement plan information plus links to articles in other facets of the wealth management industry. 

401kHelpCenter - sends a weekly email in addition to hosting a website that is a 'go-to' for timely articles on both 401(k) and 403(b) issues.  Check out their Collected Wisdom section searchable by topic (e.g. MEPs, 404(c), fees, TDFs, ESG investing, etc.). 

BenefitsLink - expertly curated news, official analysis and guidance. Great message board for vexing plan issues.

401kSpecialist - dedicated to equipping retirement plan advisors with the vision, specialized knowledge and cutting-edge technology that are vital to your success.  Sign up online for their print edition of the monthly magazine. 
 
National Association of Plan Advisors - part of our industry trade group, American Retirement Association, again with a daily email called NAPA-Net.

The American Society of Pension Professionals & Actuaries (ASPPA) and its four sister organizations, comprise the American Retirement Association. On the "Hot Topics" section of their website, you'll find terrific articles on cybersecurity, DOL Enforcement, missing participants and more.      

Between these six sources of retirement plan information, you won't miss a thing. But if you have a favorite source of plan news, I'd love to hear about it.  Email me at tobrien@nwp401k.com.
NWPS Launches their Mobile App!
NWPS is excited to announce the launch their new participant mobile app!

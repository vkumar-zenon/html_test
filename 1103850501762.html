Sheep Seldom Shiver.

Why is that you might ask? Well, it's partly because wolves can hear teeth chattering from a thousand yards, but mainly it's because sheep are well insulated with their own coat of natural, home-grown wool. When used as floor-covering, wool is not only soft and elegant underfoot, it breathes, helping keep things cool and comfortable during the hottest months and insulates keeping things warm and cozy during the colder months. In fact, wool carpet is unquestionably the best insulating material available for use as an interior textile and, as such, can greatly reduce energy costs.

BAA...BITUARY!

Speaking of sheep...this year the world lost Chris, a Merino sheep who died at age 10 in an Australian sanctuary in October. He was called a "miracle sheep" when in 2015 hikers discovered him, alone, abandoned, and struggling badly, carrying almost 90 lbs of five years of wool that would have caused his imminent death had he not been rescued.  Chris lived out his last years under the kind care of a sanctuary where he was sheared regularly and kept comfortable and safe for his remaining days. If you love animals, you'll appreciate this video about Chris...RIP, Chris.

""" News from Northeast Guild for Spiritual Formation
The Northeast Guild for Spiritual Formation is a contemplative community which encourages and supports people from all traditions or no tradition, who desire growth in their spiritual lives. We seek the presence and guidance of the Sacred by providing experiences to foster spiritual deepening. Local gatherings such as meditation groups and Quiet Days, our monthly newsletter, our on-line and in person workshops and presentations, and an annual retreat for Associates and Friends of the Guild provide opportunities for hospitality and teaching. We also offer a network for spiritual directors and resources for those desiring spiritual companionship.

Welcome to the December Newsletter
 
 
If you look up at our masthead, you'll see that we have revised our Mission Statement to clarify who we are and what we do. We wanted to emphasize that we are a contemplative community, residing not in any specific geographic location but in a shared preference for silence in an age of incessant information, mystery in an era of partisan certainties, creation in a time of mass consumption. We also wanted to give people who might be reading about us for the first time an idea of some of the opportunities we offer.

The Guild is a nonprofit organization, but we do have operating expenses. Please read the letter from the Stewardship Committee below and consider making a contribution to the Northeast Guild to support our work in bringing contemplatives together.  Donations may be made by PayPal  on our website , by setting up a monthly bank withdrawal (please call Marty at 207-812-0070), or by using the envelope enclosed with the Guild's annual appeal letter.

Finally, we hope you'll join us in welcoming Elaine Hewes as the newest member of the Board of the Northeast Guild. And as you'll also see below, we're still looking for one or two more Friends or Associates to serve along with her and the rest of us.  Since we are a 501 (c)3   non-profit organization,  we especially need someone with secretarial skills.

A Message from the Stewardship Committee



Listening creates a holy silence. When you listen generously to another they can hear truth in themselves, often for the first time. And in the silence of listening you can know yourself in everyone. Eventually you may be able to hear, in everyone and beyond everyone, the Unseen singing softly to itself and you. -Rachel Naomi Remen

Does it ever seem like no one is listening? We probably all feel that way sometimes. Perhaps that is why Rachel Naomi Remen's soothing words are so attractive to us. We are drawn to the empty chairs in the photograph which invites us to imagine who might be sitting next to us and listening.

Guild participants have learned much this year from listening. We kept hearing from Quiet Day attendees, "I can sit in silence at home alone, but it's not the same as sitting here in silence with each other." Those of us who think we don't have time to listen because we lead such busy lives were very grateful to Ann Carroll, PhD, who presented two internet discussions on the topic of "Taking a Look at the Question of Time."  She explained how our whole concept of time has changed, from the cyclical to the linear. She asked the provocative question, "What if all time is just a thought?"

We also learned new perspectives from Sherri Mitchell, author of Sacred Instructions: Indigenous Wisdom for Living Spirit-Based Change, in her "A Day of Teaching" program. She enabled us to catch a glimpse through ancient wisdom traditions to a spirituality which is whole and healing, residing in the true marriage of the sacred and the mundane. We also heard stories from Guild Associate, Dr. Daniel Robinson, in his workshop, "Writing your Spiritual Autobiography and Soul Mythology." This interactive workshop gave each of us an opportunity to write a story from our life and to listen to and be listened to by another person.

This coming year the Guild plans to extend our offerings in a new way. We have been holding an annual three-day retreat for our Guild Associates. This year we will extend the invitation to our Friends, which would be you. It will be held at the Alcyon Center on Mount Desert Island the weekend of May 22-24, 2020. Be sure to read the Guild Monthly Newsletter for more details. Once registration is open, we anticipate it will fill quickly.

We are grateful for your support and involvement. Your gift to the Guild will help fund our operating expenses. Donations may be made on our website  www.northeastguild.org, setting up a monthly bank withdrawal (call Marty at 207-812-0070), or using the enclosed envelope.

Your continued involvement helps us reach more people with the message, "Eventually you may be able to hear, in everyone and beyond everyone, the Unseen singing softly to itself and you."
 
Sincerely,
The Stewardship Committee:
Martha Resotko, Daniel Robinson, Nancy Collins


Would You Like to Serve the Guild and Come to Know it Better?

Since the NEG is  a 501 (c)3 non-profit organization, we are required by the State of Maine to have a Board of Directors that elects officers, maintains records, and meets throughout the year to conduct the "official business" of the Guild. After many years of faithful service, a couple of our Board members have stepped down, and we are currently in the process of replacing them. One of the more difficult-to-replace members was our faithful and highly efficient secretary who recorded and brought order to our discussions and kept that written record for posterity. At this time we are looking for someone who possesses those organizational skills, is able to attend 4 - 6 meetings per year (generally in the Belfast area but sometimes via Zoom), and would like to learn more about and become more involved in Guild affairs. 

Ours is a non-paid, "working board" whose members seek to  conduct our gatherings in ever more contemplative ways. We feel that we are on an adventure together, forging this next season of Guild life. If you would like to join and assist us in this way, please email Heather Spring at  prayerstudy@gmail.com.

EcoPsychology Initiative 
REALIZING POTENTIAL & SOLVING PROBLEMS WITH NATURE'S PRINCIPLES
The EcoPsychology Initiative (EPI)  offers workshops, consultations, and training based on the principles and experiences of nature. We work with the leaders of communities, organizations, businesses and people simply seeking to enrich their life. We offer a path towards doing good and doing well. In a world filled with historic opportunities, challenges, and uncertainty, our programming offers a much-needed model for how to cultivate thriving on all levels of society: people, prosperity, and the planet.


EPI offers the following:
  • Public and private programming
  • Online & In-person
  • Trainings
  • Consultations
  • Workshops
  • Speaking engagements
  • Retreats
Integrating nature's principles catalyzes positive growth, leadership and healing. Our systemic and psychological maps cultivate awareness of where you are and what brought you here, and innovate solutions for where you want to be and how to get there.  Using the natural environment as a guide, we help you better organize, engage and impact your world.

In addition to our website, we share information and engage with community on two different Facebook pages. One is our public EPI page where we share articles, ideas, etc. We also have a closed Facebook group to inspire meaningful interactions and to spark conversation and thought.

Become a Friend of the Guild
The Northeast Guild for Spiritual Formation welcomes all who sense an interest in our programs and in our vision for an inclusive community of contemplative practitioners offering mutual support for our spiritual journeys.

The Guild designates all persons who financially support our vision and our work as Friends of the Guild. Your contributions help to support our newsletter, our website, our resource library and our programs.

By making a basic membership gift of $25 or more, you become a Friend of the Guild and make it possible for us to continue to sponsor all of the above initiatives. In addition to helping to support our other endeavors, your tax-deductible gift helps underwrite Guild Institute programs so that we can continue to attract first-rate speakers and presenters to the area.

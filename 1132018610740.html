Millipedes In Your
ï»¿Home and Landscape
Millipedes are very common and beneficial arthropods in lawns and landscape beds. While they are found by the thousands practically everywhere in the landscape, they usually go unnoticed. Occasionally millipedes migrate in search for a new home (nobody knows exactly why). Such mass-migrations can be quite alarming as millipedes crawl over sidewalks, driveways, walls, and occasionally inside homes and businesses.
Luckily, millipede migrations are short-lived and these friendly visitors usually disappear within several days. Treatments targeting millipedes are typically not necessary. If millipedes venture inside, they typically die due to lack of moisture within several hours.
There is nothing that you can do to prevent millipedes from embarking on a mass-exodus. But you can limit them from venturing inside your home by implementing these simple tips:
  • Minimize gaps around doors and windows
  • Ensure irrigation is not watering your structure
  • Remove trees or shrubs branches that are in contact with your home or business.

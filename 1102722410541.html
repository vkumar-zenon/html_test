Stu dying the ancient texts of Chinese medicine: the Fall.

"Qiu", the word for "Fall" in Chinese is formed of two characters: "grain" & "fire".  "Grain", because it was the time to bring the grains in and "fire" because after the harvest was over, the farmers would burn the fields to clean them up, and help bring nutrients back in the soil.


 

The Su Wen, translated from Chinese to French by Claude Larre, and from French to English  by Peter Firebrace, says:


 

" While summer took over from spring with "Qi" that pushed the surging and development of life to its fulfillment in flowers and fruits, the Earth now undergoes an alteration, a reversal of this trend."  As the movement of the "Qi" (energy) of Spring is surging, the movement of the Qi of Summer is expanding, the movement of Fall is contracting. 

As farmers harvest the fruits of their labor, it is a time for us to finish projects we have started in the Spring and Summer. Summer is when we spend more time outdoors, socializing, traveling.  Fall is the time to reflect on the activities, personal growths of our summer.  We are getting ready for Winter, changing to more indoors activities, coming inside our body and mind. Assess, reflect, organize, (practical life as well as the life of our soul) these are words associated with the Fall.


 

The Fall corresponds to the element of Metal which is in charge of organization, setting limits, order and communication.  


 

"Prosperity apparently continues, there is an overflowing from Heaven and, at the same time, as a consequence, there is a suspense, and equilibrium in what has been produced on Earth."  This was a time in ancient China, when justice was carried out, and punishments took place (summer was too busy).  It is a time for evaluating balance in our lives, "cutting off" the surplus and unnecessary, as part of putting things in order before the winter sets in.

 


 

"This distribution of Qi must be done calmly."  This refers to the sense of urgency as things need to be in order before the harsh season.

 The ancient reminded us not to waste our precious energy in worries about what the winter might bring, but to calmly sort out, assess and trust in what we have.

 

We must not relax and layback as in the summer (the time for celebration and gathering of friends is over) but get into a busy and efficient mode.  "The peaceful direction of one's own domain with its thoughts, will, emotions and occupations of all kinds is the appropriate activity."


 

Although the daylight has been decreasing (the Yin and the Yang which were intertwined in the summer are pulling apart), the fall is a season associated with brilliance, radiance ("Ming"), a sense of having reached one's full potential.


 

"One goes to bed early, one gets up early." Living according to Nature also means varying the amount of sleep we get throughout the different seasons, something we would naturally do if we did not have electricity.

 

In Chinese Medicine, the Lungs and the Intestines are the organs of the Fall, the metal element.  The Lungs are associated with the emotion of "letting go".  If you have difficulty in letting go, this feeling will be more prominent in this season.  Extra support from your acupuncturist will help you make the transition.

Abdominal breathing and Qi Gong are practices important in the Fall.  They will help us get emotional tranquility, increase our energy, maintain physical strength and prevent diseases.

Sleep is another aspect of staying healthy in the Fall.

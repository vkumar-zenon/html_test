TIME TO STEP BACK
You may remember from your time working with us there's a lot of value in pausing, reflecting and stepping back a moment rather than stepping in. Inevitably, holidays give us some time to reflect. Even if we're busy, for most of us it's not the usual busyness of the rest of the year. Whenever we have a break from work, ideas clarify and it's not unusual to have some light-bulb moments. Useful thoughts crystallise when we give them the space to do so.

For many, the turn of the year is also an opportunity to take stock. What happened in the year that is coming to a close and what are our hopes and dreams for the year ahead. A perfect opportunity for a quick appreciative inquiry:

We mentioned Michelle Obama last month (and what she has to say about imposter syndrome) and we've also been looking at her new book which is a guided journal. The journal is based on her autobiography, Becoming which was released this time last year. It includes some points for inspiration along with some reflection - 'describe your proudest moment', for example. Trickier than it first appears (maybe), thought-provoking certainly. So, if you want to go hardcore and Mrs O appeals to you, you're catered for. If not here are some questions from us:
  • When do you feel at your best?
  • If you had a completely free day, how would you spend it?
  • What are three of your personal values and how do those play out in your daily life?
  • How would your best friend (home or work) describe you?
  • What one thing would you like to be different this time next year?
  • What can you do to move towards that becoming a reality?
  • To what are you most looking forward?

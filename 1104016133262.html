Deborah Landau Reading Wrap Up


About 50 Katonah poetry enthusiasts got together to hear and meet Deborah Landau on November 17, 2019 at the Katonah Village Library.    It was a sobering reading, her poems so focused on our current tragedies--from terrorism to climate change, not to mention our short lives! Regarding her tragedy-filled book Soft Targets, she said, "I keep wanting this book to become obsolete, but it hasn't yet..." She read from her poem "Soft Targets": "O you who want to slaughter us, we'll be dead soon enough what's the rush."    
 
The mood was subdued, but not totally somber, with a heightened intensity due to the subject matter. Landau commented later on how involved and focused the audience seemed to be.



One comment during the Q&A was that her poems sound beautiful, even though the subject is so grave. She replied, "That's how I work, by ear. I suspect there are a lot of poets in this room, are there?" Many hands raised, and she said, "I always read my poems out loud when I'm working on them. Do you?" There was much nodding in the room. We were taken by how responsible she feels for the world. Landau apologized for the heaviness that emerges from her poetry as a result, adding at the same time, she doesn't want to be "aestheticizing horror."   

 

At the very end she read a lovely poem about snow to lift us up a little. She called it a "palate cleanser."    
 
The deepest redress is a thick and fulsome snow.
Peaceful prevail of the afternoon, looking out
at this bluish marvel the air.

Are you, or is anyone you know, dreading any aspect of the upcoming American Thanksgiving holiday or of the year-end holiday season in general? The immediate goal is that we all end this week, as in the one that begins today, November 24, and ends on Saturday, November 30, 1000% more grateful than we began it, alright?

Here's how.

Using the super effective learning technique called spaced repetition, do this every day for the next 7 days and, for certain, the process will produce the result.

  1. Make a list of 10 - 15 things you're grateful for, happy about, relieved by, interested in, grooving on, stimulated by, excited for or feel calm when considering. On a scale of 1 to 10, weight the intensity of your happy; i.e. I'm 7.0 excited about Saturday's hike. This list becomes your "Conception List."
  2. Every day, starting today, read your list, aloud, 20 different times throughout your day. This super important step is what exploits the super effective "spaced repetition" technique.
  3. YES, you CAN modify your list as newly discovered grateful data points come into being - in fact, you can righteously expect much more of that throughout this process and beyond. You might end up with 4 - 7 list revisions; that's okay, there's no wrong way to do this as long as you're working the steps.
  4. In a week, make a completely new list which becomes your "Reception List" and compare it to your Conception List. Be sure to evaluate how easy or not it is to list things you're grateful etc. about, and compare your Conception to Reception numeric intensity evaluations of them.
  5. Enjoy being more grateful.
  6. Repeat, Reuse, Recycle.

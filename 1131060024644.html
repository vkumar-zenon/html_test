Dining Destination
Parma Italian Roots
I love trying new restaurants and if you haven't been to the newish (they opened a year or so ago) Parma Italian Roots, I'd recommend you head on over. In the former Cold Beers & Cheeseburgers location in Scottsdale's Grayhawk community, this fresh and trendy eatery makes me feel like I am on a mini vacation!

While the cuisine is most certainly Italian, you'll find more than just your classic Italian dishes. Here, they've married that distinctive West Coast style into a fusion of fine Italian and California cuisine. Known for their handmade pastas, house-baked breads, and Neapolitan-style wood-fired pizzas, the quality of the ingredients shines through with every bite.

Open for lunch, happy hour, dinner and Sunday brunch, Parma offers well-crafted dishes and deliciously inventive cocktails.

The dinner menu starts with snacks and small plates and meanders into greens, pastas and pizza; with a foray into their Ranch & Sea entree options.

If you're in a tapas type of mood, you and your dinner companions may be perfectly happy indulging in a handful of small plates. The Grilled Spanish Octopus, with red pepper romesco, crushed hazelnuts, fennel and Fresno chili; the Crispy Brussels with pancetta and parmesan; the Ricotta-Stuffed Squash Blossoms and trio of Bruschettas (a favorite is the Stracchino with speck ham, toasted sesame and blueberries) are an excellent complement to a shared bottle of wine.

You'll find that all of their classic pasta entrees such as Gnocchi, Linguine & Clams, Rigatoni and Pappardelle Bolognese are also available gluten-free.

As for heartier entrees, the Seared Ora King Salmon served with roasted fingerling potatoes, roasted tomato, corn and salsa verde; and the Slow-Roasted Beef Short Rib with roasted Brussels sprouts and barolo demi just might be the stars of the dinner menu.

In addition to a robust wine list with selections from Italy and California, Parma makes a mean cocktail. You might like the Old Hollywood vibe in the Mello Cello, made with vodka, Limoncello, Cointreau and egg white; and the unexpected flavors of their Primrose crafted with tequila, grapefruit, cinnamon syrup and rose. Of course if you want a cocktail that can tell its own stories, I loved the House of Secrets. Made with mezcal, green chartreuse, citrus and Maraschino liqueur, its kick comes from a finish with an absinthe rinse. Cheers!

6 Mistakes That Can Sabotage Your Home Purchase
(Do you know someone that is considering buying a home? Send this article to them! They'll be thankful for the helpful advice and happy that you thought of them.)

The only thing worse than not getting the home you wanted is not getting it because of an avoidable mistake. While working with a real estate agent certainly helps -- and we recommend it -- buying decisions are ultimately in your hands, so you want to be as prepared as possible. Here are six common mistakes people make when home shopping:
Not Being Ready To Make An Offer
Looking at properties without a pre-approval letter is like leading a toddler to a candy store knowing you forgot your wallet at home. You won't like the result.

A pre-approval letter is the document that I'll give you that explains in detail how much money you qualify to borrow and what price home you qualify to buy. It assures the seller that not only are you ready and serious about purchasing the home, you have a lender approval and a mortgage company ready to fund your loan. Submitting an offer without a pre-approval letter may also demonstrate that you don't fully understand the home sale process, which could possibly work against you in negotiations as well.

Getting pre-approved is much easier and faster than it was a few years ago too! You can get started right now using my online application. It's secure, convenient, and the quickest way to know how much you qualify for.

Hesitating Too Long Before Making An Offer
Of course, you're allowed to hesitate. It's a big financial decision, and emotions can run high. But don't let uncertainty slow you down too much.

When it comes to real estate purchases, time can stifle and even kill the deal. You see, the longer you wait, the more competitors you'll encounter that also want to buy the home. A bidding war means you'll end up paying more or even get outbid, missing out on the property altogether.

This is also where being prepared with your pre-approval letter comes in. If you're hesitating because you still need to apply for the loan, there's a high possibility that you'll miss out on that property.

Going All-In On Your First Bid
The next mistake is to put all your chips on the table --meaning offering your max pre-approved amount on the first round. What you want to do instead is look for homes that are below your maximum approved. That way, you have some wiggle room for bidding a bit higher on the second round (while others will be unable to because they didn't read this article and bid max on their first round).

These trick also helps when going against all-cash offers, too. All-cash offers have a reputation of being tough to outbid because they're attractive to sellers. But if they've gone all-in on their first bid, your higher loan-backed bid can take them out of the competition.

Going In Too Low
On the same note, you don't want to low ball either. Sometimes home shoppers make an offer to negotiate, and it backfires. Remember that your end goal is to buy the home, not to simply get into a negotiation.

Instead, your first offer should be one that is fair for the house and the market while remaining under your max bid amount. Your real estate agent can help you with this one but do your homework as well. Research similar homes in the area to see how much they sold for and how long they were on the market. Compare the features as well as the square footage as it also affects what a fair offer would be.

Passing on The Inspection Contingency
Perhaps they didn't want to ruffle the seller's feathers, or maybe they thought that newly constructed homes wouldn't have flaws. Whatever the reason may be, we've seen too many home buyers make the mistake of declining the home inspection contingency.

A home inspection contingency gives you peace of mind. It uncovers potential flaws that may require major cash to fix.

Whether it's a new home or a jewel from the 1950s, always get a home inspection.

Letting Too Many People Have a Say
This mistake is tied to the second one I mentioned about hesitating. Letting people who are not financially invested in the purchase sway you, could slow you down or make you second guess yourself. Yes, having advice from your mother or a friend is valuable, but their helpfulness may be too protective. If your "advisor" is the type to only focus on flaws or tell you "shop around" when they think you've fallen in love too fast, it might be better to shop without them.

Hot Cocoa Cookie Cups
Source: ButterWithASideOfBread.com
"My wife made these for a cookie exchange party last year and they were a hit. Thought I'd share for any of you holiday bakers out there." - Brady

Ingredients
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup softened butter
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp. vanilla
  • 1/3 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 1/2 tsp. baking soda
  • 2 cups flour
  • 2 TBSP holiday sprinkles

Fudge Filling:
  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 14 ounce can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

You'll also need:
  • 24 mini candy canes
  • tiny marshmallows*
  • 1 TBSP white chocolate chips melted

Instructions
Making the cookie cups: Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F. Spray a mini muffin tin with cooking spray.

With a hand mixer, cream together sugar and butter. Add egg and vanilla. In separate bowl, combine sour cream, salt and soda. Add flour and sour cream to butter mixture, beat until smooth. Add 2-3 TBSP holiday sprinkles and mix just until combined. Scoop dough out using a 2 TBSP cookie scoop and put one in each mini muffin cup. No need to roll the dough to make it smooth- it will all bake the same.

Bake cookies for 15-18 minutes, making sure theyâre not getting too brown at the end. {If they are, pull them out.}

Right after you take them out, use a small measuring spoon to push into the middle of each cookie cup, creating a space for the fudge filling to go. You donât want to push through the cookie completely -- but make sure you create a big enough space.

Let cool in the pan for 10-15 minutes, then gently run a thin knife along the sides to pop them out. If you greased the pan well, you shouldnât have any problems here!

To make the fudge filling: Combine the chocolate chips and sweetened condensed milk in a microwave safe bowl. Mix lightly and microwave on high for 1-2 minutes. I have a powerful microwave, so mine only took 1 minute. The mixture doesnât look melted, but give it a few gentle stirs and youâll see itâs heated well enough. Add vanilla and stir again until smooth.

Transfer about 3/4 the fudge into a quart-sized Ziplock bag. Squeeze the fudge into a corner and snip the corner off. Now you can easily pipe the fudge into the cookies! The great thing about this fudge too is that it sets up super fast, so feel free to fill the cookies well, so that the fudge goes above the cookie- it will set up and not run out! If your fudge gets too solid, just pop it back in the microwave for 10-15 seconds.

To assemble the cookies: Cut the rounded part off the candy canes and adhere them to the side of each cookie cup with melted white chocolate. This is actually real easy-- just put white chocolate on the cut portion of the candy cane, press it to the side of the cookie cup, then set it down on the counter until white chocolate is solid. After about a minute, the candy cane handle is well attached to the cookie cup!

Fill each cookie cup with fudge. Place several tiny marshmallows on top. Let cool.

Store cookies in an airtight container on the countertop, or in the fridge for up to 1 week.

*Be sure to get "tiny" marshmallows, not mini marshmallows. The tiny marshmallows can be found next to the hot chocolate. My grocery stores no longer carry the separate containers of them, so I buy the "Marshmallow Lovers" hot chocolate packets. Inside the box the hot chocolate and tiny marshmallows are in separate envelopes. You'll need about 2 envelopes for this recipe. Or, you might find them on Amazon.

Your Mortgage & Finance Questions Answered
Q: I want to refinance but a lot of people are telling me to wait until rates go even lower. How do I know when it's the right time to refinance?

A: Predicting interest rates in the future is near impossible. Rates sometimes change daily with no rhyme or reason on direction. For example, If you had asked me a year ago when fixed rates were at 5% if I expected them to drop down below 4% any time soon, I would have chuckled at that. But mortgage rates surprised us and have come way down this past year. No one saw that coming and we have no idea how long these low rates will hold. The way I see it, the right time to refinance is when it makes mathematical sense. I like to say numbers tell the truth. If we can save you money now with a very short break-even on closing costs, then today would be the right time for you to refinance. Waiting for rates to go even lower is usually a losing proposition because in my experience when rates hit bottom and go back up, we're always surprised. Think of it this way: Low rates are like a good sale. The opportunity won't last forever. Is there a chance they'll go even lower? It's hard to tell. There's an equal chance they'll rise higher. It's like playing Interest-Rate Roulette. The safe bet is to refinance if it'll save you money.

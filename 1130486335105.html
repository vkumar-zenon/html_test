Message from our President, Pat Batten:

Dear Sisters in Christ -

The holidays are upon us and we are busy with family and friends and facing a new year all too soon.  As I'm working my way through the Thanksgiving leftovers of turkey, pie and cranberry sauce, I'm reminded to be thankful for our work as Daughters of the King during this past year. 
 
In reflection, we welcomed Faith Lutheran Chapel's St. Therese of Lisieux chapter in January, new members of the Order at St Michael's and All Angels, Columbia, and St. Luke's in Newberry. The brand new St. Teresa of Avila Chapter was created and installed at Church of the Good Shepherd in Greer.  Our Spring Retreat with horses and healing as well as our Fall Assembly that brought members together to share our goals, prayers and spirits were very successful and rewarding. 
 
Now that we are celebrating Advent we look forward to Christmas and the birth of Jesus Christ.  In 2020 we will have two new chapters formed and installed with both groups starting their studies in January - one in Clemson and another in Chapin.  
 
Many blessings to all of you and may your holiday season be a safe and memorable one.
 
Always FHS,
 
Pat Batten
President

Here are the duties of the 4 elected officers:

Diocesan President:
a. Preside at all meetings of the Diocesan Assemblies and the
Executive Board.
b. Appoint a Secretary, or a Recording and a Corresponding Secretary,
Committee Chairs, and a Board Advisor.
c. Appoint a Chaplain with the consent of the Bishop.

e. Plan the program of the meetings or designate a program chair.
f. Be the representative of the Diocesan Assembly to the Province IV
Board.
g. Provide a written annual report to the Provincial Assembly. If
unable to attend the Provincial Board meeting and/or Assembly,
appoint someone to represent the Diocese at said meeting.
h. Coordinate all plans for the Annual Meetings.
i. Perform all other duties pertaining to her officer.

First Vice President:
The First Vice President shall in the absence of the President, perform
all duties of the President. In case of the resignation or death of the
President, the First Vice President shall automatically become
President. She shall encourage and assist with the formation of new
chapters. She shall act as a resource in this area for the Diocese. She
shall assist the Diocesan President as requested by her.

Second Vice President:
The Second Vice President shall in the absence of the President and
First Vice President, perform all duties of the President. The Second
Vice President shall be next in line to succeed the Presidency in case of
the resignation or death of the President and First Vice President. She
shall act as Communications/ Promotion Chair for the Diocese, and
under the direction of the President help disseminate information
about the Order. She shall assist the Diocesan President as requested
by her.

Treasurer:
The treasurer shall collect all monies of the Order in the Diocese and

Daughters, we are at the season of Advent, the beginning of a new Church year. We will learn about the prophesies of the coming of a Savior and fulfillments of those prophesies during the lives of the early Jews. It is a time of recognizing the long time of waiting that the Jews endured, praying faithfully for the coming of their Savior to rule over them. And, in time, we will learn of the true reign of our Christ, who died for all (including us!) and who reigns, not on earth, but in heaven over all. Imagine: God in human form left heaven to come to earth to die for our sins. How great is the love of God!
 
Of course, the season of Advent has been adopted commercially as a time of buying, for Christmas gifts and festivities, and buying again. Thereâs a feeling that you must have a gift for everyone whom you might meet this month. Iâve already gotten caught up in the giving and buying spirit and made a (LONG!) list of names of people to whom I will owe gifts. And, of the foods that I can make ahead for visits: apple cake for one daughter, macaroni and cheese for another, pie for the sons-in-law, pecan cookies for my brother.  It can get harder and harder to focus on giving what we can to our Lord and King! I do hope to find some time at least each week for extra prayer and thanksgiving time. Whatever your traditions are for this season, I pray that it is a blessing to you. Let us all keep our prayers constantâfor peace in the world, for blessings to the poor, for all those you love, and for a blessed Advent for all Daughters. Pray for the coming of the Lord Jesus!

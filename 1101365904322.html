Second Sight Film Screening & Discussion
Thursday, January 23, at 7:00 PM
Jacksonville Jewish Center

Come see the Jacksonville premiere of the award-winning film Second Sight. There are 18 million poor and blind people in the world whose lives could be transformed through a 10-minute sight-restoring surgery that costs as little as $25. Second Sight is a moving record of one woman's journey from blindness to sight. The screening will be followed by a lively panel discussion with: Cole Sax, the film's writer and director, Jeff Levenson MD, Chief Medical Officer, SEE International, and Rabbi Jonathan Lubliner, Jacksonville Jewish Center.

This event is free and open to all! To reserve your seat visit bit.ly/secondsightjax or call the Jacksonville Jewish Center Office at 904-292-1000.

Let's Make "House of Hope" a Home!
Construction is underway at the Youth Crisis Center!  The public spaces are being renovated to meet the needs of the new residents, but the bedrooms are still waiting to be "adopted and furnished".  Thank you to those of you who have made donations, but we still need more money to make this dream a reality. The Youth Crisis Center is estimating that the renovation will not be completed until mid-January and the rooms won't be set up until the end of January or the beginning of February. New furniture and furnishings will be purchased for all of these rooms. Plaques with the donating organization's name will be on each room for three years.  For more information, please contact Rhoda Goldstein at rhodagoldstein@hotmail.com.

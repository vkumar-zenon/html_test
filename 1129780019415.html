Are your tires rolling?
Winter is here and every year we've replaced trailer tires due to frozen brakes. Brakes can freeze to the drum in as little as 20 minutes. Make small turns to each side, check your mirrors, and ensure your tires are rolling. Or, pull up 10 feet, get out, and check for skid marks. These repairs are expensive and chew up valuable time and money. We're lucky if it's only the tires and not the aluminum rims that need replacing. Make sure your tires are rolling, every time!
Changes to our Cell Phone policy
Drivers are no longer allowed to have cellular devices, such as smart phones, smart watches, tablets, and computers, in the cabs of the vehicles they're driving for us. Trucking regulations don't allow drivers to even look at their cellular devices to read a text message. And, as long as your cellular device is in the cab of your truck, avoiding eye contact with it is, often times, too difficult.
First violation will generate a written warning.
Second violation within 12 months will result in termination.

Why did the deer cross the road?
It's November; the mule deer will start their breeding season and the elk are moving down to lower elevations for the winter. During breeding season, deer tend to follow their noses without looking both ways before crossing the road. Our trucks are MOST LIKELY to crash into deer and elk on the road during the months of November and December. The average truck repair after colliding with a deer is $5,500 and collisions with elk average $16,000. And neither of these numbers reflect lost time. Slow down at night. Scan the road farther ahead and to the sides.

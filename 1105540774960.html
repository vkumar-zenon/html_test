Francisco Middle School Implements 
E-portfolios Schoolwide!
by Lorissa Anderson, AVID Site Coordinator at FMS

The Francisco Middle School AVID team has successfully implemented e-portfolios to all students.  That's right, not just AVID students, but all 6th, 7th and 8th-grade students created their own personal e-portfolio where they will begin to collect and reflect on work in all of their classes throughout the year.  Our wonderful AVID teachers, who also teach other in other subject areas, worked together to ensure all Francisco Middle school students set up their e-portfolios. We are proud to embark on this tool that emphasizes students' ownership of work.  

In the AVID class, students utilize their e-portfolio to slowly organize their work and reflect on assignments, or classes.  Our students have begun recognizing the strengths they possess and feel proud of the work they've done in school. Goal setting has also become more profound.  Students spend time digging to the root of their struggles to then come up with their own solutions.  

This new tool reminds me of TRF's and tutorials.  The e-portfolio is similar to the TRF and the reflection tools are like mini-tutorials designed to help them problem solve how to best improve themselves as learners.  One of the best parts is that this e-portfolio grows with the student, so they are not only able to see trends in their learning within the year but year after year.  We at Francisco are very excited about this new integration and hope you're inspired as well. If you are curious about e-portfolios and would like further insight please email me: andersonL1@sfusd.edu.
Schoolwide AVID Program at San Ysidro High School


During our ADL 2 training in San Diego, Myra and Lara were able to visit San Ysidro High School, an AVID demonstration school.  Over 43% of San Ysidro High School students are designated English Learners, and 70% qualify for free or reduced lunch.  San Ysidro High school is less than 5 miles from the Mexican border crossing, and many students commute across the border every day to attend the school.

At the site visit we listened to presentations from the principal and site coordinator, and were able to hear from a panel of current AVID students.  We also were able to visit classrooms throughout the school.  While we we visiting classrooms we saw AVID WICOR strategies being used across content areas, and students active and engaged in collaborative inquiry projects.  In the AVID elective classrooms we saw students engaging in focused and lively tutorials in the humanities as well as in math and science.  The cognitive load of learning was squarely on the shoulders of students, and teachers trusted their students to lead their own learning and work in groups.

What we saw in classrooms was the result of the hard work and commitment from the teaching and administrative staff. The school had integrated EL support through AVID and Kagan strategies, and had dedicated early professional development days to AVID strategies.  All of the school's literacy and content coaches were core members of the AVID site team, and the entire staff were invited to the Site team meetings.

One of the most compelling parts of their AVID program was their AVID for Special Education Program.  The head of the school's special education department collaborated with the AVID coordinator to create this program for students in RSP who are an A to G graduation track.  RSP teachers were trained in AVID strategies and modified the curriculum as necessary.  Many of the case managers of the students enrolled in the program reported gains on student IEP goals in self-advocacy, social-emotional learning and speaking, in addition to reading and writing.

New Online AVID Tutor Training


The AVID team is pleased to announce that there is a new online course for training AVID tutors.  This course, that was originally created by former AVID TSA Veronica Reilly-Granich, is on google classroom and has a variety of videos, resources, and assignments that ensure that tutors are equipped to facilitate tutorials in your classrooms on day one.

One major advantage of moving from in person trainings to an online course is that tutors can start working on completing the training immediately after being hired.  This allows us to make sure that tutors can work in your classroom sooner. Additionally, tutors have access to the online course and materials the entire year long as a resource.

Modules for the new course include an introduction to AVID, Costa's levels of Inquiry, the AVID tutorial process, Scholar Groups, Behavior Management/Engaging Students, Professional Boundaries and Mandated Reporting, and Tutor Expectations and Procedures.  

The AVID team plans to create more online courses so that we can provide ongoing professional development to our new and returning tutors.


Staff Reflection: ADL5 Training
by Natalie Gomez



This November I was able to complete the final component of the AVID District Director training. A five part training broken down over a year (Understanding, Implementation, Certification and Expansion), the final part focuses on supporting systems that promote college and career readiness for all students. We also focused on the importance of college and career readiness in our own districts, the barriers that affect these goals, and the connections between our Impact (The Why), Contribution (The How), and New Learning (The What).

While at these trainings there are a diverse number of school staff from all over the country focusing on AVID implementation and teaching strategies, SFUSD has the unique perspective of sustainability at 17 middle and high school sites. This means focusing on understanding the challenges of each site, finding creative and supportive solutions within the larger system, and understanding the number of curriculum initiatives. As our AVID Site Coordinator at Roosevelt MS says, we see AVID not as AVID strategies, but as effective teaching strategies that produce results and can be implemented schoolwide. For example, in our first meeting with our AVID Director this year, we found that within SFUSD 92% of AVID Seniors completed their A-G requirements compared to the district rate of 58%.

It was great to get into the "why" of our beliefs about culture, equity and college and career readiness. We also reviewed two books focused on leading with change, The Speed of Trust by M.R. Covey and Motion Leadership by Michael Fullan. Both texts focused on the importance of building trust and transparency within your collaborations. One of my favorite takeaways from this exercise was focusing not on changing people, but rather in creating a system where positive change is inevitable.

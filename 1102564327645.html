Dear OYC Members, 

I know that those who were able to attend Sunday's Annual General Membership meeting heard this but there are those out there who were unable to attend and I would like to recap my opening remarks from the meeting for their benefit. 

The Olcott Yacht Club holds a special place in the hearts of many of us. It is not just a building sitting on a unique piece of property on the north end of Olcott Harbor. It is a place where its members gather to enjoy the sport of sailing and more importantly the companionship of fellow members. As a member of the "Board" for the past five years we have spent endless hours discussing high water, vulnerable infrastructure, sandbags and finances. But the real treasure of OYC is the spirit of its membership. No matter the cause of the issue facing us, our membership rallies to the crisis and gives their best to fix the problem.
 
To that point, the BOD proposed and the membership approved the levying of a $100 special assessment fee to offset our expenses for additional fuel costs associated with the high water level when we needed pumps running 24/7, losses experienced by the restaurant (despite Julie cutting $6500 in labor costs) and to offset the purchase of a new Point of Sales system for the Club. The Board worked hard to minimize the losses incurred this year and I am confident that the assessment is the best method of addressing the issue. The assessment invoices will be in the mail before the end of this year to all Senior and Associate members. We also welcome any support from members not included in the assessment.  

The last order of business was to thank the outgoing Board members; John Raskopf, Sue Cassidy, Meg Thompson, Melynda Stolzenberg and to elect the new board of directors for 2020. Elected were Mike Capen, Sue Cassidy, Jason Cramer and Roger Voss. The 2020 Board of Directors is:

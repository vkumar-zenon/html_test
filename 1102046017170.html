DRESSING SLIMMER

As years pass, it can be difficult to keep extra weight from accumulating. Too many gentlemen make the mistake of putting off an investment in their professional appearance until they "lose the weight." Here's how to improve your personal style while minimizing the visual effect of any extra girth:
  • Cut your dress shirts to fit you comfortably-excessive fabric tends to create an unsightly balloon effect while an overly tight fit limits your ability to move freely.
  • Consider V-neck sweaters instead of crew-neck, and choose thin knits rather than bulky cable knits.
  • Select clothing that is slightly more formal than needed for any given occasion-it'll put the focus on your confidence rather than your waistline.
  • Go for the distinguished look of suspenders (braces) rather than a belt, which can draw unwanted attention to the midsection.
  • Have your shirt collars shaped to stay neatly tucked under the jacket lapels or choose a hidden button-down collar, to smooth out your silhouette.
  • Avoid pairing high-contrast pieces together, since they break up the form into large color blocks. Instead, select ensembles created from similar colors to keep the vertical silhouette intact.
  • Consider cutting the lapels in your suits slightly larger than standard. This creates a sense of gravitas and importance by emphasizing the torso.

THE ELEMENTS OF COLOR

Learning about the elements of color can help you select fabrics more deliberately, whether you aim to evoke feelings or make a personal statement.
  • Hue is the general name of the color (red, blue, yellow, green, etc.)
  • Value, or tone, is the color's relative lightness or darkness. If it's lighter than the true hue, it's called a tint. If it's darker, then it's called a shade.
  • Chroma, or intensity, describes the brightness. Colors can range from muted to true hues to vivid. This aspect of a color gives it a feeling of personality and helps to convey an emotion.
  • Achromatic means it's without color, and can include only black, white, and gray.
With custom clothing, you have complete control over all these elements of color in each wardrobe item.

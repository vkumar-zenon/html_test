Having grown up in a household of music and musicians, I was immediately intrigued. I remember the deep sense of calm and wellbeing that I experienced while cuddled beneath the grand piano in our living room. It could be Chop-Sticks or Moonlight Sonata that someone was playing. It didn't much matter. I loved the resonant tones that sprang from the keys striking the strings and reverberating off the sound board. I could lie there for hours letting the vibrations dance though me. So, I thought yes, I can relate to idea of healing and relaxation being generated by sound.
Lolly at 6 months playing the piano w/ Dama
It certainly didn't occur to me as a child, but I've since come to learn that sound has been used for healing by many cultures and indigenous peoples for thousands of years. According to Sound Healer Nate Martinez, this is how it works: " Sound helps to facilitate shifts in our brainwave state by using entrainment. Entrainment synchronizes our fluctuating brainwaves by providing a stable frequency which the brainwave can attune to. By using rhythm and frequency, we can entrain our brainwaves and it then becomes possible to down-shift our normal beta state (normal waking consciousness) to alpha (relaxed consciousness), and even reach theta (meditative state) and delta (sleep; where internal healing can occur)." Click here read What You Need To Know About Sound Healing by Nate Martinez.


Sound Healing is used to treat anxiety and depression. And the benefits can include pain management, decreased mood swings, lower blood pressure, improved sleep and lower risk of coronary artery disease and stroke. That seems like a bunch of great reasons to lie down on the floor and let sound waft over you.

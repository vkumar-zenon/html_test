Vicar's Voice
Parish family, friends and community,

Thanksgiving is an "omni-temporal" reality and certainly at Christmastide when we make the effort to be little more excessive in all manner of parish life, including the liturgies. I am grateful to everyone who made the parish life special at this time of the year. Thanks to all who gave gifts to the children we adopted for gift giving. Thanks to those who organized the effort to make it special for the kids and their families.

A special thanks to Pam for organizing and directing the inter-generational Christmas Pageant. We understand the improvisation of the the tiny angels and shepherds; we not sure what to do with the improvisation of grey bearded angels and kings. Good thing they have "day jobs." But Pam gets an award for "herding" cats.

Thanks to all who participated in our liturgy as creche players, lectors, liturgists, singers, and instrumentalists. We are grateful that Rod and Greg accepted the draft of their mom to play bells and flute and sing. Thanks for Colin's solo too as well as the Bells of St. Mary's and the Adult choir. We appreciate Josh's playing and direction, assisted by Pete and Rob. We enjoyed the Troubadours offering on 4 Advent. Then there was the altar guild, the greeters, and those who greened the church. And many more.

And as a comic once said, "80% of life is showing up." Thanks to everyone who showed up to share in our community celebrations of Christmas. We hope that you have been blessed in being renewed in the meaning of the birth of Christ. We hope that doing Christmas together as a parish family has reinforced us in knowing ourselves as part of the Holy Family. Mary, Joseph and Jesus have allowed a host of people to be a part of the Holy Family and it was a delight to share it at St. Mary's-in-the-Valley in 2019.

We have one more Christmas liturgy on Sunday.

9:30 a.m. December 29, Lesson and Carols
 
I wish you a glorious 2020.


In Christ,

Father Phil

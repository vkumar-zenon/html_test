Compost this Winter
Curious about composting all your food scraps at home like we do in our lunchroom? (A family of four generates on average 20 to 30 lbs. of compost each week!) Make a difference in the fight against climate change and sign up for the Winter Gap Composting Program, offered by locally-owned Collective Resource. This 16-week program allows you to pay a one-time fee to have a composting bucket dropped off at your residence for weekly or bi-weekly pickup.

Leave the Leaves
Did you know that raking and leaving the leaves on the ground or in plant beds can help you suppress weeds, act as natural fertilizer, and provide a habitat for insects and other critters? Skipping leaf blowers can be good for your wallet and for the environment. See what the National Wildlife Federation has to say about the benefits of leaving the leaves HERE and find out how you can help Natural Habitat Evanston support wildlife right in your own backyard.

I can't believe it's that time of year again, and neither can you. It rolls slowly toward us until it's a massive snowball ready to run us over - or maybe that's just how I feel. I'm thrilled to be presenting our last couple of author events in December, and then getting ready to beat last year's speed-gift-wrapping record of 32 seconds. 

A terrific local journalist named  Madeleine Wells interviewed me for an article she wrote for S.F. Gate about  mid-century food pamphlets , and how disgusting the holiday foods they presented were. It is a laugh-out-loud funny article, so I hope you'll take a few moments to read it here .

The past month was  packed with author events , from Sean Brock and Fuchsia Dunlop, to Najmieh Batlangij, Alison Roman, Apollonia Poilane, and many more renowned authors and chefs. We learned about Appalachian foodways, Szechuan peppers, regional Iranian cooking, and famous European bread bakers. We sampled Thai green curry, chard and goat cheese tarts, Baiju (a Chinese liquor with an acquired taste), and local cheeses. We peaked with our respective Thanksgiving dinners, then slept it all off. If you missed all of this, not to worry;  signed cookbooks make wonderful presents ! I always make sure to order more than I need so I can have lots of signed books on hand to sell through the holidays, and you can find and order them all  here !

Aside from out two December author talks with the editors of the new  Joy of Cooking  (Dec. 5) and  Toni Tipton-Martin  (Dec. 12),   Bravetart  author Stella Parks  will stop by at 11:00 a.m. to sign her popular baking tome on Dec. 2, and  Loyale Studio  will put on a pop-up of their ethical kitchen essentials (like aprons) outside the shop on Sunday, Dec. 8. That day, we'll also be selling signed cookbooks off-site at  The Progress Holiday Market  from 11:00-2:30 p.m.

Of course, I always love  vintage books on food , and have recently   added a large number of them   to my website. There are books on canning from WWII, coffee from Colombia, farming in British East Africa, bananas from the Philippines, and just about everything in-between. They are a fascinating window into our global food history, and researching them to write descriptions is what keeps my engine humming. 

Thank you all for a wonderful year so far, and please come in and let us find you the perfect gift for someone special (or just an office pool - we love choosing something for them, too). 

Sincerely,
Celia Sack

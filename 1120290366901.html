Monday Night Torah with Phil Lazzar
12/30/19, 6pm-7:30pm
TBE Library

Dedication

The name Chanukah means dedication and it refers to when the Maccabees re-dedicated the Holy Temple some 2,200 years ago.  Some 200 years later, that Temple was destroyed by the Romans. Our sages teach us that the reason the Romans were able to destroy the Temple was due to baseless hatred between ourselves.

I think that when anyone feels hatred, they never think it is baseless, they can always say the reason.  However, I think our sages are saying that most of the time the hatred is really baseless.

Furthermore, our sages teach us that our homes are deemed to be mini-Holy Temples and that at this time of Chanukah we are to purify and re-dedicate them.  So, what is it that needs purifying? They teach that the thing that defiles our homes the most is anger. Self-righteous anger.

Our sages also teach that each person's heart is like a micro-mini-Holy Temple.  Here too, we need to purify our hearts of self-righteous anger. We need to bring peace into our hearts, our homes, and ultimately our world.  But it begins in our own hearts and then expands to our homes and after this it expands to our communities.

What is the source of self-righteous anger?  Our sages say it is from arrogance and egotism.  When a person lives a good life and does good to help others and treats everyone with respect, they can sometimes feel a certain entitlement to having things go their way.  And when they don't, they feel that things are not fair and become frustrated and end up on the spectrum of anger.

This anger spectrum runs from simple annoyance to the other extreme of rage.  Being anywhere on this spectrum is a sign, an indicator of arrogance and egotism.  To my way of thinking, the lack of peace in the home is the greatest impurity in the world today and that correcting this should be our number one priority.

Without peace in the home, we will not have peace in our communities nor will we have peace in the world.  We can put in effort to fix all the world's other problems but if we don't fix this one problem we're still living in the other place, the place that start with an "h" but is not heaven.

The Hebrew word for peace is SHALOM.  The word shalom includes the concept of wholeness, completeness.  It is not a struggle free peace but rather it is a peace that comes through the struggles.  The struggles are not in the way of shalom, they are actually the only way to reach shalom when they are done in the right way and aiming for the right target.

STOP the BLEED Training - 3/10/20
As part of our security updates, Caroline Connor M. D. will be doing a training on March 10th, 2020 from 6-8 pm  at  Temple Beth El.  If you are interested in this, please RSVP to Blue at  bethel@reninet.com or  707-444-2846  

During Stop the Bleed training, you will learn how to:
  • Determine if an area is safe for you to proceed toward a victim to provide assistance
  • Identify any nearby tools to assist you such as a publicly placed bleeding control kit or everyday items that can be used to control bleeding.
  • Use your hands to apply direct pressure at the site of the wound to stop bleeding
  • Pack a deep wound with cloth or gauze to control bleeding
  • Correctly apply a tourniquet to an injured limb to stop bleeding

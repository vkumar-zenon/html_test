ASD Myths Debunked
Recently, it has come to our attention that there may be some misconceptions among our staff in terms of understanding how our company and system operates. To help shed light on these issues, we are pleased to introduce a new ASD Myth Busters section to our newsletter.

Myth #3: Working From Home = More Calls
When ASD first began offering our experienced employees remote computers to work from home, it was to minimize the risks associated with traveling into the office during hazardous weather events. Home computers also helped support employees while recovering from personal injuries that made it difficult to leave their home. As time went on, ASD staff demonstrated their ability to use these stations conscientiously and the decision was made we would allow employees to utilize their home stations on a more regular basis. 
Today, we now have 159 remote computer stations. Some people might believe those who are answering calls at home are forced to answer more calls than they would if they were scheduled to work in the office. There is no truth to this myth. ASDâs system does not and has never differentiated between home and in-house employees when assigning incoming phone calls. When working in the office, it is very easy to determine when the call volume is higher due to the sound of other Operators in the room handling calls. While working at home, this frame of reference does not exist, and we believe this is the reason there is a misconception that those working at home are given more calls. 

If you have a home computer, it means you have demonstrated you can be trusted to reliably handle calls without direct, in-house supervision. Working at home is meant to be a privilege, not a punishment. It is an incentive that offers you more flexibilityâ¦not more phone calls!

How the Czachor Family Grew ASD Into the Company It Is Today 
After 47 Years, the Czachor Family has learned a lot of about running a funeral home answering service. Recently, ASDâs 2nd generation Family-Member Owners were interviewed for by a magazine called the Funeral Business Advisor. The Professional Spotlight feature included information about our company's roots, goals for the future, and the Czachor family's personal business philosophies. The feature also included some great family photos of the family throughout the years.

Kathy, Marty and Kevin spoke about the enduring influence of their parents and shared insights about how ASDâs specialized training and technology has allowed our company to grow from a locally-based call center to the largest funeral home answering service in operation. The feature also included personal bios where Kathy, Marty and Kevin each had an opportunity to share their own personal beliefs and experiences. Below is a portion of the published interview with a link you can click to read it in its entirety.
How did ASD get started? Where does the story begin?

Kevin:  In 1972, our parents, Martin and Barbara Czachor, decided to start a telephone answering business for local companies in their area. My mother is a perfectionist and is all about mutual respect. She has always made it a priority to make our staff and callers feel respected. She found very quickly that funeral directors fit that criteria perfectly. They looked for quality, they were always respectful to our staff, and they sold value.

With the technology that was available to them, they were only able to serve their local community and hometown of Glenolden, Pennsylvania. There werenât enough funeral directors nearby to sustain an answering service exclusively for the funeral profession. Then in the mid-1990s, toll free service became available and that changed up the world. It allowed us to reach more people and as a family we decided that we were going to exclusively reach out to the funeral profession. At that point, we were working with about 300 funeral homes locally and now we serve nearly half of the funeral homes in the United States.

Tradition
We brought back an old tradition to St. Ignatius Loyola School. On December 6, the Feast of St. Nicholas, students put their shoes outside of their classrooms; the shoes were magically filled with a candy cane and a card with a prayer to St. Nicholas. The students squealed with delight when they discovered their treats!

Service

This robot is not only mobile and programmable, but can be used in an increasing number of ways as its user progresses. Hour of Code

As part of a global movement, our students participated in the Hour of Code. Each student was able to find an activity that both interested them and challenged them. The Hour of Code initiative was created to demystify the concepts of coding and to show that coding is something that every student can do. This 21st-century educational skill crosses all borders and knows no boundaries. We are proud that our students became a part of such a transformative experience.
It's a Beautiful Day in KB!
We are very proud of Miss Ouchis, who, since late summer, has been participating in a program called Educators' Neighborhood, a pilot program through the Fred Rogers Center at Saint Vincent College in Latrobe, Pennsylvania. Throughout the school year, fourteen educators from various locations around the country, will join together to share and learn from each other about the impact Mister Rogers is having on students. The Institute is selecting and documenting episodes of Mister Rogersâ Neighborhood that the teachers then show in their classrooms.

During the weeks that Miss Ouchis has been showing Mister Rogers' Neighborhood, her students have been fascinated by "Picture, Picture", which shows them how certain items are made. Their imagination has grown through the Neighborhood of Make Believe and they are learning how to deal with situations that may occur in their lives. Many of these topics give the children the opportunity to broaden their understanding through classroom discussions. Situations and topics that have been watched and discussed include going
to a new school, the importance of taking time to be quiet, and how to deal with mad feelings. Through Mister Rogers' focus on the child, the classroom slows down every Friday afternoon from their fast-paced day. The children are drawn to the calm and quiet personality that Mister Rogers exudes. He engages them and makes them feel as if they were the only child in the room.

Miss Ouchis is thankful for the opportunity to participate in this program though her alma mater, and is excited to learn from other neighbors in the neighborhood! Won't you be our neighbor?

2nd Grade
Mrs. Williams' second grade opened a thank you box sent to them from Okinawa, Japan. In late October, the students sent happy birthday cards to a unit of special Marines. The Marines were so moved by the kindness of these young students that they responded with personalized notes to each student along with a small bag of special treats. The 2nd graders listened carefully as each note was read aloud to the class. The notes included information about the Marines' lives in Japan and how 'motivated' they were by the thoughtfulness. By the look on the students faces, happiness was shared by all.

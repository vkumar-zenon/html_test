Top 10 Reasons Why Music is an Effective Tool in Therapy
Music challenges the brain to listen for patterns and differentiate tones and sounds, such as how many beats you can hear on the drum. This requires attention, focus, and organizing the information heard.

Below are 10 ways music is used in therapy at Rise to help our kiddos develop and grow!

1.   Music is a core function in our brain . Our brain is primed early on to respond to and process music. Research has shown that day-old infants can detect differences in rhythmic patterns. Mothers across cultures and throughout time have used lullabies and rhythmic rocking to calm crying babies. From an evolutionary standpoint, music precedes language. 

2.   Music enhances learning.  Do you remember how you learned your ABCs? Through a song! Classroom Spotlight: Bears!
We have a very sweet and fun group of kiddos in the Bears classroom this year! They have learned so many new things since the beginning of the year, including separating easily from family members at drop off, sitting in a regular chair to eat at the table, taking a nap on a mat, pushing their own chair to Circle Time, and feeding themselves with a spoon. 

Friendships are blossoming, and the Bears are beginning to learn to share their space and toys with each other.

Recently, the Bears have learned about fall, Halloween, and pets. The Pets theme was by far the favorite! The children learned that pets need food, water, love, and trips to the vet. It was so much fun to see them take care of their stuffed animal pets. They even took them for walks around the school on a leash. In December, we will begin learning about holidays.

Industrial Hemp Ordinance - Board Action
Received from Greg Plucker
Community Development Director Colusa County 530-458-0480
Good Morning All,
I wanted to let you know the results of the Board meeting yesterday on the industrial hemp ordinance amendment. During the meeting a concern was discussed about potential cultivation impacts to nearby off-site residences given that there is no setback requirement in the ordinance. During the development of the ordinance this issue was considered by the Ad Hoc Committee and at the time no recommendation was developed. However, during the meeting yesterday the entire Board discussed the issue, as well as the Countyâs right-to-farm ordinance, and the Board ultimately decided to continue the matter to January 7, 2020 and directed to staff to develop a setback recommendation.
Given the Boardâs discussion I have drafted an additional provision to address this issue. Given your past interest in industrial hemp operations, I wanted to know if this additional provision would impact your previous plans. If you could let me know at your earliest convenience I would greatly appreciate it.
Existing Residence Setback.
In addition to the 1.5 mile community setback requirement, there would also be a setback from an existing off-site residence owned and/or occupied by inhabitant that is not associated with the cultivation operation as follows:
500 foot setback for 0 to less than 10 acres of cultivated area.
750 foot setback from 10 acres to less than 20 acres of cultivated area.
1,000 foot setback for 20 acres or more of cultivated area.
If you could let me know if this additional provision would impact your planned operations I would greatly appreciate it. - Greg Plucker

The Fish and Game Commission (Commission) heard testimony regarding its proposed Delta Fisheries Management policy and Striped Bass policy at its meeting this week. The draft policy recognizes the importance of managing Delta fisheries in a way that improves habitat for listed species and alleviates threats rather than simply promoting populations of Striped Bass. The goal of the policy shift is to allow

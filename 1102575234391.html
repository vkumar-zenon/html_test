City of San Mateo Updates

New Web Pages Highlight Parking in San Mateo
Say hello to the City's new  Parking and Transportation web pages ! Now you can find the most current information about electric vehicles and motorcycle parking, where to repair your bike, and locations of Downtown parking facilities right from the City's website. From the new page, you can also visit the  Downtown Parking Permit page to learn about the permit and apply. 

Reminder: Parking Downtown is FREE after 6pm every day, on Sundays, and on City-designated holidays.  

Sandbags
Our winter storms have arrived! The City's Public Works teams has made sure there are free sandbags if you need them. You can pick them up at E. 3rd Ave. and Anchor Rd., or at the Corp Yard at 1949 Pacific Blvd. Both sandlots are maintained throughout the winter with sand and bags. You need to bring your own shovel to these  Self-Serve locations. We ask you to limit 10 bags per vehicle, and be San Mateo residents. Find all the details you need  here.

New Year Brings Minimum Wage Increase
The minimum wage in San Mateo will increase to $15.38 starting January 1, 2020. To help balance the rising cost of living in the Bay Area, in 2016 the San Mateo City Council adopted an ordinance approving a local minimum wage that is higher than the state and federal minimums. The City's ordinance went into effect in 2017. On January 1, the minimum wage in the City will be $15.38, then increase annually according to the regional Consumer Price Index. For information on the City of San Mateo Minimum Wage, contact  the City Manager's Office at (650) 522-7153.

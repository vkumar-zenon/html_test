Making Dreams Come Alive

Sean Brevig is a 25-year-old with big dreams. One goal is to audition for American Idol as a  solo singer in 2020.  "I want to show  who I am and show the disability community 
that I, who am also disabled with cerebral  palsy, I did it! That I accomplished it."

Sean's family likes to sing, music was always around, and he too "loves to sing- all genres - opera, jazz, R&B, folk, rap, blues, soul, bluegrass."

He's taking Communications class at College of Marin, performs with a friend during Sunday Open Mic Night at Wu Wei Tea Temple in San Anselmo, writes reggae and hip hop with another musician friend. Sean says, "I'm a single songwriter and freestyle rapper. My first song, Shot, is on my Facebook page, Lean on Sean. I was saddened to hear my friend's dad was shot in front of him on the street."

With Casa Allegra's support he shares an apartment with his good friend Graham, who he's known since preschool. "They have a special bond, something we can't describe," said Eri, Sean's mom. "We couldn't ask for more than he has now at CACS. Love that the staff meet the individual's needs. Sean and Graham enjoy a variety of events and activities in the community with their staff Alex and Javier."

Eri said, "It was a tough change for Sean." But Sean said, "Then I began laughing and making jokes" with Graham and staff. "It's a pretty fun house. I like that there are a lot of different people in my home." With Graham, "We watch TV, listen to music and we dance, in our wheelchairs too."

Eri said that Sean has learned to "Focus on what you CAN do, not what you can't do. So many things you don't realize you can do. It's a scary thing anytime you do something for the first time, then you figure it out."
